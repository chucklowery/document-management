# System Governance Principles

These principles are normative for all downstream design.

## Human Comprehension Governs Presentation

Structural completeness shall not be achieved at the expense of human comprehension. Complexity shall be progressively disclosed, and consequential machine-readable structures and automated actions shall have usable human explanations.

## Attention Is a Governed Resource

Notifications, propagation, approvals, dependency alerts, and automated recommendations shall be governed to prevent unbounded interruption and alert fatigue.

## The System Must Survive Its Implementations

Content, identities, Revision history, Relationships, Trails, provenance, manifests, annotations, and governance metadata shall be exportable in documented, independently interpretable forms.

## The Shared Graph Does Not Own Context Meaning

The shared graph owns cross-context identity, addressability, and relationship conventions. Bounded contexts retain ownership of language, invariants, lifecycle transitions, and authoritative records.

## Automation May Propose but Shall Not Silently Redefine Meaning

Automated agents shall not silently redefine terms, alter authority, resolve disagreement, adopt consequential updates, remove acknowledged work, or convert inference into fact. Automated contributions shall identify the agent, governing instruction, inputs, material tool or model version, and limitations.

## Consequential Change Must Be Explainable

A consequential change shall record intent, rationale, affected assumptions, expected impact, authority, and relevant dependencies, not merely a content difference.

## Information Has Stewardship Cost

Information shall not be retained, linked, classified, propagated, or made authoritative without regard to maintenance burden, sensitivity, staleness, expected value, and responsible stewardship.

## Constitutional Principles Must Be Operationalized

Every normative principle shall trace to policies, stories, requirements, acceptance evidence, verification results, or an approved time-bounded exception. Uncovered principles shall be identified as unimplemented.
