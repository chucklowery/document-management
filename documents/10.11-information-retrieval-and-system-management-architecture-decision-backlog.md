# Information Retrieval and System Management Architecture Decision Backlog

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

These decisions are required to implement the Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management Amendment.

## ADR-IR-001 — Retrieval Corpus, Index, and Ranking Architecture

**Status:** Backlog

Decide how Corpus Definitions, Searchable Units, Index Revisions, Ranking Policies, result explanations, multiple valid views, and replaceable retrieval engines are represented and evaluated.

## ADR-IR-002 — Change Event, Ordering, and Consistency Architecture

**Status:** Backlog

Decide event envelopes, idempotency scope, ordering scope, checkpoints, retries, dead-letter behavior, replay, backfill, read-your-writes boundaries, and Consistency Projections.

## ADR-IR-003 — Connector Contract and Synchronization Architecture

**Status:** Backlog

Decide Input and Output Contract representation, mapping versions, authentication, rate limits, source checkpoints, quarantine, correction, deletion, and external reconciliation.

## ADR-IR-004 — Output Manifest, Subscription, and Delivery Architecture

**Status:** Backlog

Decide Output Manifest persistence, delivery idempotency, acknowledgement, retries, expiration, supersession, withdrawal, subscription evaluation, and channel adapters.

## ADR-IR-005 — Relevance Evaluation and Feedback Governance

**Status:** Backlog

Decide Evaluation Set governance, judgment scales, precision and recall measures, ranked-result metrics, safety and diversity measures, adoption gates, online experimentation, feedback use, and rollback.

## ADR-IR-006 — Derived-Store Authorization and Revocation

**Status:** Backlog

Decide pre-index versus query-time enforcement, authorization partitioning, field and region filtering, facet and count protection, vector and embedding controls, policy-version handling, cache invalidation, and removal verification.

## ADR-IR-007 — Concurrent Change and Reconciliation

**Status:** Backlog

Decide Expected Base Revision enforcement, mergeable fields, text and Relationship conflict rules, multi-parent Revisions, human and agent conflict handling, offline synchronization, and Reconciliation Records.

## ADR-IR-008 — Operational SLO, Degraded Mode, and Recovery Architecture

**Status:** Backlog

Decide service objectives, telemetry ownership, alerting, capacity controls, degraded-mode transitions, backup boundaries, RPO, RTO, recovery exercises, and incident evidence.

## ADR-IR-009 — Historical Retrieval and Temporal Indexing

**Status:** Backlog

Decide recorded-time and effective-time retrieval, late-arriving information, historical index reconstruction, temporal relationship selection, and reproducibility of historical result sets.

## ADR-IR-010 — Derived-State Reconstruction and Infrastructure Replaceability

**Status:** Backlog

Decide how indexes, backlinks, caches, Projections, subscriptions, and delivery state are reconstructed from canonical envelopes, manifests, events, and policy records without retaining implementation-specific identity.

## Recommended Order

1. ADR-IR-002 and ADR-IR-006 establish safe change and authorization boundaries.
2. ADR-IR-001 and ADR-IR-009 establish retrieval semantics and historical behavior.
3. ADR-IR-003 and ADR-IR-007 establish input and concurrency behavior.
4. ADR-IR-004 establishes output delivery.
5. ADR-IR-005 establishes empirical relevance governance.
6. ADR-IR-008 and ADR-IR-010 establish operating and replacement confidence.
