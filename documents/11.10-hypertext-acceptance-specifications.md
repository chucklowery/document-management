# Hypertext Acceptance Specification Extension

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document extends `11-acceptance-specifications.md` with observable scenarios for the Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment.

# Feature AS-010 — Bidirectional Relationship Discovery

**Stories:** US-036

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-001, REQ-HT-002

## Scenario AS-010.1 — Discover inbound citation

**Given** Resource A cites an exact Revision of Resource B,

**And** the user is authorized to inspect both Resources and the Relationship,

**When** the user views inbound Relationships for Resource B,

**Then** the system displays the citation from Resource A,

**And** identifies the Relationship Type and applicable Revision.

## Scenario AS-010.2 — Inbound discovery does not transfer authority

**Given** a Source Owner can see that a destination includes the source,

**When** the Source Owner inspects the inbound Relationship,

**Then** the system does not grant authority to modify the destination unless separately authorized.

## Scenario AS-010.3 — Restricted backlink does not leak

**Given** a restricted destination references a visible source,

**And** the user lacks permission to know that the destination or Relationship exists,

**When** the user inspects inbound Relationships, counts, graph paths, or errors,

**Then** the system does not disclose the restricted destination or inference-enabling metadata.

# Feature AS-011 — Trail Authoring and Comparison

**Stories:** US-039, US-040, US-041

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-006 through REQ-HT-011

## Scenario AS-011.1 — Create Trail Revision

**Given** a Trail Author has selected source objects,

**When** the author records an ordered path for a stated audience and purpose,

**Then** the system creates an immutable Trail Revision,

**And** records ordered steps, target references, selection modes, rationale, and known limitations.

## Scenario AS-011.2 — Material Trail change creates new Revision

**Given** Trail Revision 1 exists,

**When** the author materially changes ordering, source selection, audience, purpose, annotation, alternatives, or stated omissions,

**Then** the system creates Trail Revision 2,

**And** preserves Trail Revision 1.

## Scenario AS-011.3 — Alternative Trails coexist

**Given** two authors create different legitimate paths through the same evidence and decisions,

**When** both Trails are recorded,

**Then** the system preserves both Trails,

**And** does not silently designate one as universally canonical.

## Scenario AS-011.4 — Compare Trails

**Given** two Trail Revisions exist,

**When** an authorized user compares them,

**Then** the system identifies differences in source selection, exact Revisions, ordering, annotations, alternatives, and stated omissions.

## Scenario AS-011.5 — Adopt exact Trail Revision

**Given** a Trail Revision is ready for organizational use,

**And** a Party has Trail Adoption Authority,

**When** the Party adopts the Trail,

**Then** the system records the exact Trail Revision, adopting scope, authority, purpose, effective time, conditions, and recorded time,

**And** does not represent the Trail as authoritative outside that scope.

# Feature AS-012 — Derivative and Omission-Aware Provenance

**Stories:** US-038, US-042, US-044

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-005, REQ-HT-012 through REQ-HT-014

## Scenario AS-012.1 — Derivative is visibly distinct

**Given** a summary is generated from authoritative source Revisions,

**When** the summary is displayed,

**Then** the system identifies it as a derivative representation,

**And** exposes its material source references,

**And** does not present it as the authoritative source.

## Scenario AS-012.2 — Synthesis records material omissions

**Given** a Synthesizer intentionally excludes a material source from a comparison,

**When** the comparison is finalized,

**Then** the system permits the exclusion and rationale to be recorded,

**And** prevents an unjustified completeness claim.

## Scenario AS-012.3 — Restricted information creates uncertainty without unsafe disclosure

**Given** relevant information is restricted,

**And** disclosure of its existence would itself be unsafe,

**When** a user views a synthesis or answer,

**Then** the system avoids claiming completeness,

**And** expresses uncertainty according to policy,

**And** does not reveal the restricted information or its existence beyond authorization.

# Feature AS-013 — Governed History

**Stories:** US-043

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-015, REQ-HT-016

## Scenario AS-013.1 — Apply governed withdrawal

**Given** a Resource must be withdrawn under an authorized policy,

**When** the authorized action occurs,

**Then** the system records action, authority, policy, scope, effective time, and permitted accountability residue, if any,

**And** prevents unauthorized future presentation of withdrawn content.

## Scenario AS-013.2 — Erasure policy permits no identifying residue

**Given** an applicable policy requires removal of identifying residue,

**When** erasure is completed,

**Then** the system does not retain prohibited identifying linkage,

**And** related references respond according to policy without fabricating replacement content.

## Scenario AS-013.3 — Projections handle unavailable historical content

**Given** source content becomes unavailable under policy,

**When** a current Projection is recalculated,

**Then** the Projection identifies an unresolved or unavailable dependency where safe to disclose,

**And** does not continue to quote or expose removed content.

# Feature AS-014 — Portability

**Stories:** US-045

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-017, REQ-HT-018

## Scenario AS-014.1 — Export independently interpretable graph data

**Given** an authorized Portability Steward requests an export,

**When** the export is produced,

**Then** it contains permitted identities, Revision history, Relationships, Trails, provenance, manifests, annotations, and governance metadata,

**And** includes documented semantics sufficient for independent interpretation.

## Scenario AS-014.2 — Export preserves Trail structure

**Given** an authorized export includes a Trail,

**When** the export is reconstructed independently,

**Then** Trail identity, Revision history, ordered steps, target identities, selection modes, annotations, and provenance can be recovered.

## Scenario AS-014.3 — Export enforces governance

**Given** some related content is restricted from export,

**When** an export is generated,

**Then** the system omits or transforms that content according to policy,

**And** does not bypass sensitivity, consent, retention, legal, or deletion restrictions.

# Feature AS-015 — Automated Contribution Provenance

**Stories:** US-046

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-019, REQ-HT-020

## Scenario AS-015.1 — Record automated contribution context

**Given** an Automated Agent creates a consequential summary,

**When** the summary is recorded,

**Then** the system records agent identity, delegated authority, instruction or policy reference and version, purpose, material constraints, material inputs, relevant tool or model version, limitations, and produced provenance.

## Scenario AS-015.2 — Protect restricted instructions

**Given** a governing instruction contains security-relevant or restricted information,

**When** an ordinary reviewer inspects the automated contribution,

**Then** the system exposes an authorized instruction or policy reference and sufficient audit metadata,

**And** does not disclose restricted instruction content.

# Feature AS-016 — Explain Consequential Change

**Stories:** US-047

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-021

## Scenario AS-016.1 — Explain more than a diff

**Given** a consequential Project Revision changes approved scope,

**When** the change is finalized,

**Then** the system permits intent, rationale, affected assumptions, expected impact, authority, dependencies, source evidence, and superseded scope to be recorded,

**And** preserves the textual or structural diff as supporting rather than sufficient explanation.

# Feature AS-017 — Constitutional Coverage

**Stories:** US-036 through US-048

**Requirements:** REQ-HT-023

## Scenario AS-017.1 — Identify uncovered principle

**Given** a normative constitutional principle exists,

**When** traceability is reviewed,

**Then** the project identifies linked policy, stories, requirements, acceptance specifications, verification evidence, or an approved time-bounded exception,

**And** marks the principle as unimplemented when none exists.

## Automation Priority

Automate first:

1. AS-010.1 and AS-010.3 — backlink discovery and leakage prevention;
2. AS-011.1 through AS-011.4 — Trail creation, revision, coexistence, and comparison;
3. AS-012.1 through AS-012.3 — derivative and completeness behavior;
4. AS-015.1 and AS-015.2 — automated contribution provenance;
5. AS-017.1 — constitutional coverage check.

Governed-history and portability scenarios require pilot-specific policy and format decisions before complete automation.
