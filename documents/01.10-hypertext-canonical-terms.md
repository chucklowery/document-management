# Hypertext Canonical Terms

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

This document extends `01-domain-glossary.md`. Terms defined here are canonical and shall be incorporated into future consolidated glossary revisions.

## Relationship Assertion

A consequential Relationship treated as an authored or generated assertion with explicit semantics, provenance, authority basis, applicable versions, and owning-contract lifecycle rules.

A Relationship Assertion does not become true merely because it exists, is central, is frequently cited, or is generated by an automated system.

## Backlink

An authorization-aware reverse discovery result showing that another Resource, Revision, Content Region, Immutable Record, Projection, or Trail has a managed Relationship directed toward the inspected object.

A Backlink is a Projection over managed Relationships. It does not transfer ownership, authority, or mutation rights.

## Trail

A versioned Resource that expresses an authored, ordered reading or reasoning path through Resources, Revisions, Relationships, Immutable Records, and Projections for a stated audience and purpose.

A Trail is not a folder, search result, workflow, or universal source of truth.

## Trail Revision

An immutable Revision of a Trail defining its audience, purpose, ordered Trail Steps, selection modes, rationale, known omissions, limitations, and governing policy references.

## Trail Step

An ordered component of a Trail Revision that references a target and may contain annotation, transition explanation, expected reader action, visibility rule, or alternative-path designation.

## Trail Adoption

An Immutable Record stating that an authorized Party adopted an exact Trail Revision for a defined scope, purpose, effective period, and set of conditions.

Trail Adoption does not establish universal truth or authority outside its stated scope.

## Canonical Source

An authoritative source within a defined context and purpose. Canonical Source status does not imply that one view, presentation, Trail, summary, or interpretation is canonical.

## Derivative Representation

An assembly, adaptation, summary, annotation, Projection, generated output, rendered output, recommendation, or automated answer produced from or about source material.

A Derivative Representation shall remain distinguishable from its sources and shall preserve material provenance.

## Epistemic Status

A classification describing how a consequential claim is known or asserted. Examples include Observation, report, inference, hypothesis, Finding, Recommendation, Decision, Projection, generated output, verification result, Intended Outcome, and Observed Outcome.

Epistemic Status is distinct from approval, popularity, authority, and confidence.

## Contextual Authority

Authority that applies only within an explicit purpose, organization, jurisdiction, effective period, bounded context, decision scope, or policy basis.

Contextual Authority shall not be generalized beyond its declared scope.

## Omission-Aware Provenance

Provenance that records not only included sources and transformations but also material selection rules, ordering decisions, exclusions, unavailable information, restricted information, stale information, unresolved information, assumptions, parameters, and relevant tool or model versions.

## Completeness Claim

An assertion that a synthesis, Trail, Projection, graph view, report, or generated answer includes all materially relevant information within a stated scope.

A Completeness Claim shall not be made when relevant information is materially unavailable, restricted, excluded, stale, or unresolved.

## Completeness Disclosure

A policy-governed explanation of whether completeness can be established. Its wording and detail are constrained by authorization, privacy, and inference-risk policy.

## Governed History Action

An authorized withdrawal, redaction, access revocation, consent revocation, retention expiry, legal deletion, anonymization, cryptographic erasure, or Relationship withdrawal applied according to policy.

## Accountability Residue

The minimum historical or audit information permitted or required to remain after a Governed History Action. Some policies may require no identifying residue.

## Automation Delegation

An explicit grant of authority permitting an Automated Agent to perform defined actions under stated purpose, scope, constraints, governing instruction or policy reference, and review requirements.

## Constitutional Coverage

The traceable relationship between a normative constitutional principle and one or more domain policies, User Stories, Requirements, Acceptance Specifications, verification results, architecture decisions, or approved time-bounded exceptions.

## Constitutional Exception

An approved, scoped, justified, and time-bounded departure from a constitutional principle that records authority, risk, compensating controls, affected artifacts, review date, and expiration.
