# Literate Operations Context Extension

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document assigns ownership for contract evolution, processing boundaries, operational narratives, drift repair, and channel retirement without creating a universal operational data model.

## Ownership

### Producing Bounded Context

Owns the meaning, invariants, lifecycle, authority, and supported Contract Revisions of information it produces.

### Consuming Bounded Context

Owns the behavior it requires from received information, its compatibility constraints, adoption decision, and evidence that a new Contract Revision is safe for its use.

### Integration and External Systems

Owns Channel Catalog coordination, connector transport behavior, source-to-contract mapping, Canonical Envelopes, synchronization checkpoints, and migration execution records. It does not redefine source meaning.

### System Operations and Delivery

Owns Processing Boundaries, Batch Outcomes, Backpressure State, admission behavior, consistency audits, Drift Findings, Repair Runs, delivery operations, and operational service evidence.

### Identity, Access, and Governance

Owns the policies that constrain channel registration, disclosure, retention, migration, retirement, operational evidence, and authorized tracing.

### Shared Graph Foundation

Owns durable identity and Relationship conventions used to connect contracts, channels, attempts, findings, repairs, outputs, and deliveries across contexts.

### Owning Context for an Operation

Owns the Operational Narrative for that operation. Documentation tooling may render, index, validate, and test references without acquiring domain authority.

## Cross-Context Contracts

A cross-context contract shall identify:

- producer and consumer identities;
- semantic owner;
- supported Contract Revisions;
- compatibility and negotiation policy;
- temporal and ordering semantics;
- idempotency and checkpoint boundaries;
- failure and partial-success behavior;
- governing service objectives;
- migration and retirement policy;
- trace and evidence identifiers.

## Boundary Rules

1. A centralized catalog provides visibility but does not centralize domain authority.
2. A connector translates between published contracts and shall not depend on another context's private model without an explicit exception.
3. Operational dashboards and indexes are Projections.
4. A Repair Run changes derived state only through the authority declared by its plan.
5. An Operational Narrative explains a workflow across contexts but does not grant mutation authority across those contexts.
6. Contract adoption and channel retirement remain explicit decisions of affected owners.
