# Hypertext User Story Extension

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document extends `08-user-stories.md` with stories derived from the Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment and Trail domain model.

## US-036 — Discover Inbound Relationships

**As a Source Owner,** I want to see authorized inbound citations, inclusions, derivations, contradictions, Trails, and publications that depend on my Resource, so that I can assess downstream impact.

**Owning context:** Shared Graph Foundation / Knowledge and Provenance

### Acceptance examples

- Given a managed Relationship, authorized users can discover it from both source and target perspectives.
- Restricted relationships do not leak through counts, identifiers, gaps, or errors.
- Discovery does not grant mutation authority over the destination.

## US-037 — Choose Adoption Mode

**As a Destination Owner,** I want to select Pinned, Live, Approval-Controlled, or Historical-As-Of source adoption, so that reuse behavior is explicit.

**Owning context:** Document Authoring and Composition

### Acceptance examples

- A destination records its selection mode and applicable source identity.
- Approval-Controlled updates require explicit adoption.
- Historical-As-Of resolution records the applicable time boundary.

## US-038 — Distinguish Derivative from Source

**As a Reader,** I want to distinguish source content from adaptation, annotation, summary, projection, and generated material, so that I do not mistake a derivative for authoritative source.

**Owning context:** Document Authoring and Composition / Knowledge and Provenance

## US-039 — Author a Trail

**As a Trail Author,** I want to create a versioned ordered path through Resources, Revisions, Relationships, Records, and Projections, so that an audience can follow a stated reading or reasoning path.

**Owning context:** Knowledge and Provenance

### Acceptance examples

- A Trail records audience, purpose, ordered steps, selection modes, rationale, and limitations.
- Material changes create a new Trail Revision.
- Alternative Trails may coexist.

## US-040 — Compare Alternative Trails

**As a Reader,** I want to compare alternative Trails and their source selections, ordering, annotations, and omissions, so that I can understand different legitimate interpretations.

**Owning context:** Knowledge and Provenance

## US-041 — Review and Adopt a Trail

**As a Trail Adoption Authority,** I want to review and adopt an exact Trail Revision for a defined scope, so that organizational use is explicit without establishing universal truth.

**Owning context:** Knowledge and Provenance / Identity, Access, and Governance

## US-042 — Inspect Omission-Aware Provenance

**As a Reviewer,** I want to inspect material sources, transformations, selection rules, exclusions, unavailable information, and limitations behind a synthesis, so that I can assess its completeness claim.

**Owning context:** Knowledge and Provenance

## US-043 — Govern Historical Withdrawal or Erasure

**As a Resource Steward,** I want to apply an authorized withdrawal, redaction, access revocation, retention expiry, anonymization, or deletion policy, so that historical integrity remains compatible with legal, consent, safety, and retention obligations.

**Owning context:** Identity, Access, and Governance

### Acceptance examples

- The action records applicable authority and policy.
- The remaining accountability residue, if any, follows policy.
- References and projections handle unavailable content without unsafe disclosure.

## US-044 — Avoid False Completeness

**As a Reader,** I want a graph, Trail, synthesis, or answer to avoid unjustified completeness claims, so that restricted, excluded, stale, or unresolved information is not mistaken for absence.

**Owning context:** Knowledge and Provenance / Governance

## US-045 — Export for Independent Reconstruction

**As a Portability Steward,** I want to export authorized identities, Revisions, Relationships, Trails, provenance, manifests, annotations, and governance metadata in documented formats, so that the system can survive replacement of its implementation.

**Owning context:** Integration and External Systems / Shared Graph Foundation

## US-046 — Inspect Automated Contribution Provenance

**As a Reviewer,** I want to identify automated contributions and inspect their authority context, instruction or policy reference, material inputs, version, limitations, and provenance, so that automation does not silently redefine meaning.

**Owning context:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## US-047 — Understand Consequential Change

**As an affected stakeholder,** I want a consequential change to record intent, rationale, affected assumptions, expected impact, authority, dependencies, and superseded scope, so that I can understand more than a textual diff.

**Owning context:** Owning domain context / Shared Graph Foundation

## US-048 — Govern Attention

**As an Attention Policy Owner,** I want notifications, dependency alerts, adoption requests, approvals, and automated recommendations governed by policy, so that users are not overwhelmed by unbounded interruption.

**Owning context:** Identity, Access, and Governance

**Priority:** Later capability unless required by the first pilot.
