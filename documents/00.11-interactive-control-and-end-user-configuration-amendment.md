# Interactive Control and End-User Configuration Amendment

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System  
**Status:** Ratified  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-27  
**Ratification basis:** Direct instruction to add behavioral requirements and commit them to `main`

## 1. Purpose

This amendment adds constitutional behavior for people who configure, operate, and adapt a system with numerous changing inputs and outputs.

It applies an interaction-design and end-user-programming lens inspired by Brad A. Myers's published research themes: visible system state, direct manipulation, rapid feedback, error prevention, recoverability, and tools that let non-specialists define useful behavior without hiding the consequences of that behavior.

This is an interpretive design lens. It does not claim authorship, endorsement, or representation by Brad A. Myers.

## 2. Scope

This amendment governs:

- user-visible system and configuration state;
- configuration drafts, validation, approval, activation, and retirement;
- end-user-authored rules, mappings, filters, subscriptions, and transformations;
- impact preview, simulation, example-based testing, and dry runs;
- staged activation, progress, pause, cancellation, resume, rollback, and compensation;
- stable bulk-selection scope and execution-drift detection;
- dependency and blast-radius explanation;
- configuration comparison, restoration, sharing, and approval;
- manual overrides, safe defaults, confirmations, and expiration;
- recovery of interrupted configuration work;
- progressive disclosure and equivalent accessible control paths.

It does not require one visual language, workflow engine, rules engine, interface toolkit, or deployment architecture.

## 3. Constitutional Principles

### IC-P-001 — The Controllable System Has a Visible System Image

An authorized user shall be able to determine which configuration is active, which inputs and outputs it affects, which mode the system is in, which work is pending, and which actions are currently available.

A control surface shall not imply that a requested action is complete merely because the request was accepted.

### IC-P-002 — Interaction Feedback Is Immediate but Finality Is Explicit

A user action shall receive prompt acknowledgement and an intelligible predicted or current effect. Long-running or asynchronous work shall expose progress, checkpoints, delay, partial outcomes, and terminal disposition.

Immediate feedback may describe intent or provisional state; it shall remain distinguishable from durable acceptance and completed downstream effects.

### IC-P-003 — Reversible Interaction Preserves Durable History

A consequential action shall provide undo, rollback, restoration, or an explicit compensation path when policy and external effects permit.

Reversal shall create new accountable state. It shall not erase acknowledged history, external delivery, or evidence that the original action occurred.

### IC-P-004 — End-User Configuration Is Governed Programming

A rule, mapping, filter, transformation, subscription, or automation authored through a user interface is executable behavior and shall be versioned, validated, attributable, testable, reviewable, and bounded by authorization.

Ease of authoring shall not exempt behavior from contracts, ownership, safety policy, or operational limits.

### IC-P-005 — Consequences Are Previewed Before Broad Activation

A consequential configuration change shall support impact preview, representative examples, simulation, or dry run when practical. Broad or high-consequence activation shall support staging, verification, and rollback or compensation.

When preview is unavailable or materially incomplete, the limitation and increased confirmation or approval requirement shall be explicit.

### IC-P-006 — Scope, Mode, and Work State Are Stable and Recoverable

Selection scope, temporal boundary, authorization context, operating mode, and configuration Revision shall remain visible and stable from preview through execution unless an authorized change is disclosed and accepted.

Interrupted authoring or execution shall be resumable from an identified checkpoint when safety permits.

### IC-P-007 — Progressive Disclosure Preserves Plural Paths and Exact Detail

Control surfaces may simplify common work, but an authorized user shall be able to inspect exact identities, Revisions, contracts, dependencies, policies, and outcomes that determine behavior.

A wizard, dashboard, or ranked recommendation shall remain one interaction path rather than the sole representation of system structure.

### IC-P-008 — Errors Are Prevented, Localized, and Repairable

The system shall validate as early as practical, identify the exact field, rule, example, item, dependency, or policy that prevents safe execution, preserve valid work, and offer an authorized repair path.

Generic failure messages, silent coercion, and destructive reset are insufficient for consequential configuration work.

## 4. Required Operationalization

These principles shall be operationalized through:

- canonical terms and a domain model;
- bounded-context and role ownership;
- user stories and EARS requirements;
- Given/When/Then acceptance specifications;
- architecture decisions and implementation constraints;
- task-based usability, safety, and operational verification;
- machine-readable constitutional coverage.

## 6. Relationship to Existing Principles

This amendment extends, and does not replace, previewable management, recoverable exploration, immutable history, explicit authority, bounded-context ownership, governed inputs and outputs, observable propagation, accessible interaction, measurable operations, plural views, and replaceable infrastructure.

Where immediate feedback conflicts with durable completion, both states shall be shown. Where undo conflicts with immutable history, reversal shall be modeled as a new action. Where simplified configuration conflicts with domain ownership, the interface shall consume published contracts rather than acquire private domain semantics.
