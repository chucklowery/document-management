# Stewardship Cost User Stories

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document extends `08-user-stories.md` with dedicated stories for ownership, staleness, downstream impact, and responsible retirement.

## US-055 — Assign Stewardship Responsibility

**As a Resource Owner,** I want every authoritative or operationally consequential Resource to have an accountable steward and review expectation, so that information does not become ownerless or silently stale.

**Owning contexts:** All owning bounded contexts

### Acceptance examples

- Stewardship identifies scope, responsible Party, effective period, and review policy.
- Shared or inherited stewardship is explicit.
- Loss of an active steward is visible rather than silently ignored.

## US-056 — Detect and Review Staleness

**As a Knowledge Steward,** I want potentially stale Resources, Relationships, Trails, policies, and projections identified for review, so that outdated information is not treated as current without examination.

**Owning contexts:** Owning bounded context; Shared Graph Foundation

### Acceptance examples

- Staleness is derived from explicit policy and source facts.
- A stale Projection does not rewrite the source Resource.
- Review may reaffirm, revise, supersede, withdraw, archive, or retain the item.
- Staleness does not automatically mean incorrectness.

## US-057 — Assess Downstream Stewardship Cost

**As a Source Owner,** I want to understand the downstream dependencies, review burden, sensitivity, and propagation cost of a Resource or Relationship, so that maintenance decisions account for organizational impact.

**Owning contexts:** Shared Graph Foundation; owning bounded context

### Acceptance examples

- The assessment identifies authorized inbound dependencies and affected scopes.
- Cost indicators distinguish measured facts from estimates or policy-derived projections.
- Restricted dependencies do not leak through totals or explanations.
- Stewardship cost does not itself determine truth or retention.

## US-058 — Retire Low-Value Information Safely

**As a Resource Steward,** I want to archive, withdraw, consolidate, or retire low-value information under policy, so that the system does not accumulate unmanaged content and relationships indefinitely.

**Owning contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance; owning bounded context

### Acceptance examples

- The action records rationale, authority, dependencies, retention policy, and resulting reference behavior.
- Affected destinations receive governed impact information where required.
- Historical or identifying residue follows applicable policy.
- Retirement does not silently erase acknowledged work or fabricate replacement content.
