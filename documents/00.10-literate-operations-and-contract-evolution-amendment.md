# Literate Operations and Contract Evolution Amendment

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System  
**Status:** Ratified  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-27  
**Ratification basis:** Direct instruction to apply and commit the amendment to `main`

## 1. Purpose

This amendment applies a Donald Knuth-inspired literate-programming perspective to system operation. Consequential behavior shall be explained in the order people need to understand it and connected to the contracts, state transitions, executable checks, and evidence that implement it.

The amendment adds behavior required to operate numerous changing input and output channels without silent contract breakage, ambiguous partial processing, unrecoverable checkpoints, hidden overload, unexplained drift, or documentation that diverges from executable behavior.

It extends rather than replaces the Project Constitution, the Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment, the Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management Amendment, and the Findability and Information Architecture Amendment.

## 2. Principles

### LO-P-001 — Narrative and Executable Behavior Form One Specification

Every consequential operation shall have a versioned Operational Narrative that explains purpose, authority, inputs, outputs, invariants, state transitions, failure modes, observability, and recovery in a human-comprehensible order and links to the machine-readable contracts and executable checks that govern it.

A narrative is not proof that behavior exists. Executable behavior is not adequately specified merely because code exists.

### LO-P-002 — Contract Evolution Is Explicit

Input Contracts, Output Contracts, event schemas, mappings, and consumer expectations shall evolve through identified Revisions, compatibility classification, negotiation or selection rules, migration plans, and declared adoption boundaries.

A producer shall not silently reinterpret an accepted payload, and a consumer shall not be forced onto an incompatible contract without an explicit migration or exception.

### LO-P-003 — Processing Boundaries and Partial Outcomes Are Declared

Batch, stream, synchronization, and delivery operations shall declare their atomicity, ordering, checkpoint, retry, and partial-success semantics.

A mixed outcome shall identify which items succeeded, failed, were skipped, were quarantined, or remain unresolved. A summary status shall not conceal item-level failure where item-level effects are consequential.

### LO-P-004 — Checkpoints, Idempotency, and Replay Make Change Recoverable

Long-running or restartable processing shall preserve resumable checkpoints, idempotency scope and retention, applicable contract and processing definition Revisions, and replay boundaries sufficient to continue or reconstruct processing without silent duplication or omission.

### LO-P-005 — Backpressure and Overload Preserve Truth

When producers, processors, consumers, or delivery channels exceed safe capacity, the system shall expose backlog and Backpressure State and apply an explicit admission, throttling, prioritization, expiration, or rejection policy.

Overload shall not silently drop acknowledged work, weaken authorization, reorder information outside declared scope, or claim current completeness.

### LO-P-006 — Drift Is Detected and Repairable

The system shall support governed comparison of authoritative state with indexes, caches, projections, replicas, subscriptions, and delivery state. Material divergence shall produce an explainable Drift Finding and, when repaired, a durable Repair Run record.

### LO-P-007 — End-to-End Operational Lineage Is Navigable

An authorized steward shall be able to trace a consequential change from source input through validation, mapping, accepted Revision, Change Events, processing attempts, derived representations, Output Manifests, and Delivery Attempts using stable correlation and causation identities.

### LO-P-008 — Deprecation and Sunset Preserve Consumers

A contract, connector, channel, format, or processing definition shall not be retired without identifying affected authorized consumers, replacement or export path, notice boundary, migration evidence, unresolved exceptions, and final sunset state.

### LO-P-009 — Time Semantics Are Explicit

Inputs, outputs, events, checkpoints, and narratives shall preserve applicable source time, observed time, effective time, recorded time, timezone or offset, precision, and known clock uncertainty where these distinctions affect meaning, ordering, deadlines, or historical reconstruction.

## 3. Required Operationalization

These principles shall be operationalized through canonical terms, a domain model, context ownership, roles, user stories, EARS requirements, acceptance specifications, architecture decisions, opinion articles, and machine-readable constitutional coverage.
