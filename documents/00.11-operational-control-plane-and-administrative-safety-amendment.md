# Operational Control Plane and Administrative Safety Amendment

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System  
**Status:** Ratified  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-27  
**Ratification basis:** Direct instruction to apply additive behavioral requirements to `main`

## 1. Purpose

This amendment establishes the human-operable management plane required to administer many changing input, processing, retrieval, subscription, output, and delivery channels safely from day to day.

The existing Constitution and ratified amendments define durable information, retrieval, propagation, contract evolution, recovery, and findability. This amendment adds the controls through which authorized people and automated agents inspect effective configuration, preview consequences, stage and reverse change, control runtime state, remediate exceptions, rotate credentials, enforce retention, manage scoped authority, and maintain trustworthy recipient preferences.

## 2. Scope

This amendment governs:

- effective configuration and configuration history;
- impact preview, approval, activation, rollout, and rollback;
- channel enable, pause, drain, resume, and disable behavior;
- routing, fallback, fan-out, and loop prevention;
- remediation of failed, quarantined, stale, or exceptional work;
- credential and secret-reference lifecycle;
- retention, deletion, legal hold, and erasure execution;
- administrative scope, separation of duties, and emergency access;
- alert policies, maintenance windows, ownership, and runbooks;
- configuration drift, export, import, and replacement;
- recipient preference, consent, and delivery continuity;
- findability, accessibility, credibility, and auditability of administrative controls.

It does not require one administration interface, configuration language, orchestration platform, secrets manager, alerting product, or deployment topology.

## 3. Constitutional Principles

### MP-P-001 — Effective Configuration Is Inspectable

The system shall make the effective configuration of a managed capability inspectable as a versioned, attributable Projection. An authorized steward shall be able to identify contributing configuration layers, precedence, environment, owner, activation boundary, unresolved references, and material drift.

### MP-P-002 — Consequential Change Is Previewable, Staged, and Reversible

Before a consequential control-plane change takes effect, the system shall expose its expected scope and effects. Governance shall be proportional to consequence, and activation shall support an explicit rollback or compensating path unless an approved record explains why reversal is impossible.

### MP-P-003 — Runtime State Transitions Are Explicit

Enable, pause, drain, resume, disable, and retire are distinct managed state transitions. Each transition shall identify admission behavior, treatment of acknowledged and in-flight work, checkpoint expectations, authority, reason, time, and resulting state.

### MP-P-004 — Routing and Fallback Preserve Policy

Routing, fan-out, failover, and fallback shall use versioned policy. A fallback shall not silently weaken authorization, consent, integrity, authority, retention, delivery semantics, accessibility, or service commitments. Cycles and duplicate recipient-visible effects shall be prevented or explicitly governed.

### MP-P-005 — Exceptions and Remediation Are Managed Work

Failed, quarantined, conflicted, stale, or partially processed items that require human judgment shall become findable, assignable Remediation Cases. Manual action shall preserve source evidence, authority, reason, item-level outcome, and any resulting Correction Revision.

### MP-P-006 — Credential Lifecycle Is Explicit

Connectors and delivery channels shall use governed secret references rather than exposed secret values. Credential issue, validation, rotation, overlap, revocation, expiration, failure, and emergency replacement shall be observable and auditable without disclosing protected values.

### MP-P-007 — Retention, Deletion, and Legal Hold Are Executed and Evidenced

Retention, deletion, legal hold, consent withdrawal, and erasure policies shall be translated into bounded execution plans across authoritative and derived representations. The system shall preserve evidence of scope, outcome, unresolved residue, and lawful exceptions without retaining prohibited content merely for audit convenience.

### MP-P-008 — Administrative Authority Is Scoped and Emergency Access Is Time-Bound

Administrative actions shall be limited by explicit resource, channel, environment, organization, tenant, or other Administrative Scope. Break-glass authority shall be exceptional, time-bounded, purpose-limited, monitored, and subject to post-use review.

### MP-P-009 — Alerts Lead to Accountable Action

An alert shall identify a versioned detection rule, affected scope, severity, evidence, owner, expected response, suppression or grouping policy, and applicable runbook. The system shall distinguish actionable alerts from informational observations and shall expose alerting gaps and excessive noise.

### MP-P-010 — Drift and Portability Are Visible

The system shall detect material divergence between declared and effective configuration. Authorized export, comparison, and import shall preserve stable identities and semantics while excluding or safely referencing protected secrets.

### MP-P-011 — Recipient Preferences and Consent Propagate

Delivery preferences, accessibility needs, quiet periods, channel choices, consent, and withdrawal shall be versioned and applied to applicable subscriptions, routing, fallback, and pending delivery within an explicit propagation objective.

### MP-P-012 — Administrative Experience Is Findable, Accessible, and Credible

Authorized operators shall be able to find controls, understand current state, distinguish proposal from effect, preview consequences, recover from navigation or action errors, and obtain accessible explanations. Administrative views shall not imply success, completeness, safety, or currency without supporting evidence.

## 4. Required Operationalization

These principles shall be operationalized through canonical terms, a management-plane domain model, bounded-context ownership, roles, user stories, EARS requirements, Given/When/Then acceptance specifications, proposed architecture decisions, machine-readable coverage, and opinion articles that preserve material tensions.

## 6. Relationship to Existing Authority

This amendment extends, and does not replace, the Project Constitution or the Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management, Findability and Information Architecture, and Literate Operations and Contract Evolution amendments.

Where continuity conflicts with safety, fallback is allowed only when it satisfies the same applicable policy boundary; otherwise the fail-closed requirement governs. Where deletion conflicts with immutable history, prohibited content shall be removed or rendered irrecoverable while a permitted non-content tombstone or execution record preserves accountability. Where centralized administration conflicts with bounded-context authority, the management plane shall coordinate through published contracts rather than silently acquiring domain authority.
