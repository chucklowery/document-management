# Operations and Recovery EARS Requirements

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## REQ-OP-001 — Operational Service Objective Catalog

**EARS:** The system shall maintain identified Operational Service Objectives or explicit pilot limits for availability, query latency, accepted-change latency, indexing delay, propagation delay, delivery delay, capacity, backup, restore, recovery, and reconstruction where applicable.

**Stories:** US-068, US-073, US-074

## REQ-OP-002 — Operational Measurement Provenance

**EARS:** When operational health is reported, the system shall identify the Measurement Definition, collection boundary, time window, generated time, and whether each value is measured, estimated, projected, or unknown.

**Stories:** US-068

## REQ-OP-003 — Capacity Limit Visibility

**EARS:** When an operating limit for Resources, Revisions, Relationships, events, queries, users, connectors, subscriptions, or deliveries is approached or exceeded, the system shall expose the condition to an authorized operator and apply the declared admission or degradation policy.

**Stories:** US-068, US-073

## REQ-OP-004 — Explicit Degraded Mode

**EARS:** When a capability cannot meet its safety or service commitments, the system shall declare a Degraded Mode identifying affected capability, start time, reason, permitted operations, stale or unavailable representations, user-visible limitations, owner, and exit criteria.

**Stories:** US-073, US-075

## REQ-OP-005 — Safe Behavior During Partial Failure

**EARS:** While a required durability, authorization, authority, or integrity dependency is unavailable, the system shall fail closed for affected consequential writes and disclosures and shall not silently downgrade policy.

**Stories:** US-072, US-073

## REQ-OP-006 — Backup and Recovery Plan

**EARS:** The system shall maintain a versioned Recovery Plan identifying durable sources, backup boundaries, encryption and credential dependencies, reconstruction order, Recovery Point Objective, Recovery Time Objective, owners, and verification procedure.

**Stories:** US-074

## REQ-OP-007 — Recovery Exercise

**EARS:** When a Recovery Exercise is performed, the system shall record the environment, source boundary, start and completion times, restored capabilities, actual data loss if any, achieved recovery measures, failures, and supporting evidence.

**Stories:** US-074

## REQ-OP-008 — Rebuild Derived Structures

**EARS:** When an authorized rebuild is initiated, the system shall reconstruct selected indexes, backlinks, caches, materialized Projections, or delivery state from permitted durable sources and shall record definitions, boundaries, checkpoints, failures, and completion evidence.

**Stories:** US-067, US-074

## REQ-OP-009 — Incident Record for Material Breach

**EARS:** When an Operational Service Objective, security boundary, durability guarantee, or recovery objective is materially breached, the system shall create an incident record containing scope, detected time, affected information or capabilities, containment, authority, resolution, and follow-up actions.

**Stories:** US-068, US-072, US-074

## REQ-OP-010 — Recovery Does Not Reassign Identity

**EARS:** When information or derived state is restored or reconstructed, the system shall preserve durable identities and shall not create new identities for the same acknowledged domain objects solely because storage was replaced.

**Stories:** US-074
