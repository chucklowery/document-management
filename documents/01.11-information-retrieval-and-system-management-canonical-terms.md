# Information Retrieval and System Management Canonical Terms

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document defines terms introduced by the Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management Amendment. These terms describe product and domain behavior without prescribing an engine, database, queue, cache, or deployment topology.

## Retrieval Terms

### Query

An expression of an information need submitted within an authorization, task, corpus, and temporal context.

### Query Session

A bounded interaction containing Queries, refinements, result views, selections, and permitted feedback for a task.

### Corpus Definition

A versioned definition of the Resources, Revisions, Regions, Relationships, records, fields, contexts, and temporal boundaries eligible for retrieval.

### Searchable Unit

The smallest independently returnable and relevance-assessable unit, such as a Resource, exact Revision, Content Region occurrence, Immutable Record, Trail Step, or Projection.

### Index Revision

An identified state of a derived Index created from a known Corpus Definition, source boundary, extraction rules, policy versions, and generation process. It is not authoritative source.

### Ranking Policy

A versioned policy that orders or groups eligible results using declared relevance factors, tie-breaking, diversification, and constraints.

### Retrieval Projection

A derived result set produced from a Query, Corpus Definition, Index Revision or source boundary, Ranking Policy, authorization context, and generated time.

### Relevance Explanation

An account of the principal factors that caused a Searchable Unit to be included, excluded, grouped, or ordered.

### Relevance Judgment

A contextual assessment that a Searchable Unit was relevant, partially relevant, not relevant, redundant, or otherwise useful for a Query and task. It does not establish truth or authority.

### Retrieval Evaluation Set

A governed collection of representative Queries, tasks, authorization contexts, candidate corpora, and Relevance Judgments.

### Ranking Regression

A material degradation in retrieval effectiveness, safety, latency, diversity, freshness, or explainability relative to an approved baseline.

### Saved Search

A versioned Resource containing a Query or template, Corpus Definition reference, filters, temporal policy, result or notification preferences, owner, and governance.

### Change Digest

A Projection summarizing permitted changes since a checkpoint, prior review, or temporal boundary.

## Input and Change Terms

### Input Contract

A versioned contract defining how an input channel authenticates, validates, maps, timestamps, deduplicates, authorizes, and records information.

### Connector

A controlled adapter that exchanges information according to an Input Contract, Output Contract, or both.

### Synchronization Run

A durable record of a bounded synchronization attempt, including source boundary, checkpoint, contract versions, counts, times, outcome, failures, and resulting changes.

### Change Event

A durable or reproducible record that managed information changed in a way that may require downstream processing.

### Idempotency Key

A source-scoped value used to determine whether repeated delivery represents the same intended operation.

### Ordering Scope

The identity boundary within which Change Events must be processed in a defined order.

### Quarantine Record

A durable record of input that could not be accepted safely, including source, contract, validation findings, integrity reference, time, retry eligibility, and stewardship.

### Replay

Authorized reprocessing of durable Change Events or source records using an identified definition and boundary.

### Backfill

Authorized processing of historical or previously unprocessed source information to establish or repair derived state.

### Consistency State

An explainable Projection of the relationship among authoritative state and derived stores or deliveries, such as Current, Processing, Delayed, Failed, Rebuilding, Unknown, or Policy-Restricted.

### Propagation Lag

The measured or estimated interval between a qualifying source change and its required effect in a named derived representation or channel.

## Output and Delivery Terms

### Output Contract

A versioned contract defining output semantics, authorization, format, delivery, acknowledgement, retention, and failure handling.

### Output Manifest

An immutable or append-only record identifying a material output, exact source boundary, definition, policy and parameter versions, generated time, completeness, integrity, and delivery scope.

### Delivery Attempt

An immutable record of an attempt to deliver an output, including channel, destination, idempotency information, time, outcome, acknowledgement, and failure details.

### Subscription

A versioned Resource identifying recipient, triggering Query or event, output form, channel, frequency, attention policy, effective period, and acknowledgement requirements.

### Delivery State

An explainable Projection over Delivery Attempts and acknowledgements, such as Pending, Delivered, Acknowledged, Retrying, Failed, Expired, Superseded, Withdrawn, or Unknown.

## Concurrency and Recovery Terms

### Expected Base Revision

The exact Revision against which a proposed change was prepared.

### Reconciliation Record

An immutable record explaining how conflicting contributions were merged, retained separately, rejected, superseded, or deferred.

### Operational Service Objective

A measurable target for availability, latency, propagation, capacity, durability, recovery, reconstruction, or another operational characteristic.

### Degraded Mode

A declared condition in which capabilities are unavailable, delayed, stale, read-only, or reduced while safety and user-visible limitations are preserved.

### Recovery Point Objective

The maximum targeted amount of acknowledged information loss measured backward from a disruption.

### Recovery Time Objective

The maximum targeted time to restore a defined capability after a disruption.

### Recovery Exercise

A durable verification activity demonstrating restoration, reconstruction, replay, policy restoration, or failover behavior.

## Usage Rules

1. A Search Result identifies its Searchable Unit rather than only copied text.
2. A Retrieval Projection is not authoritative source.
3. A current Index Revision is not called complete unless policy permits.
4. A Relevance Judgment is not truth, approval, or authority.
5. A Change Event does not replace authoritative state.
6. Delivery does not imply comprehension, acceptance, or adoption.
7. Degraded Mode is visible to affected authorized users.
