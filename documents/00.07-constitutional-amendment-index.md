## Normative Rules

1. The Project Constitution and ratified amendments together form the constitutional authority for downstream artifacts.
2. A ratified amendment extends or constrains the Constitution without silently replacing unaffected principles.
3. Where two constitutional provisions conflict, the more specific ratified provision governs within its stated scope unless a later amendment states otherwise.
4. Stories, requirements, acceptance specifications, architecture decisions, implementations, projections, and opinion articles do not override constitutional authority.
5. Every ratified amendment shall be reflected in language, domain models, context ownership, roles, stories, requirements, acceptance specifications, and architecture decisions where applicable.
6. Constitutional coverage shall be machine-readable. Missing coverage is recorded as unimplemented rather than assumed satisfied.
7. An associated opinion article may explain a material tension and named counterpoints but does not waive either valid requirement.
