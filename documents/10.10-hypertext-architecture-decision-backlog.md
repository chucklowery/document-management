# Hypertext Architecture Decision Backlog

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

These decisions are required before the first production implementation increment.

## ADR-HT-001 — Durable Identity Strategy

Decide identifiers and durability guarantees for Resources, Revisions, Relationships, Content Regions, Trails, Trail Steps, Immutable Records, and Projections.

## ADR-HT-002 — Canonical Semantic Serialization

Decide the documented semantic model and lossless open or documented serialization used by textual and visual editors.

## ADR-HT-003 — Content Region Anchoring

Decide how regions survive edits, movement, splitting, merging, and conflicting revisions without silently targeting the wrong content.

## ADR-HT-004 — Relationship Persistence and Lifecycle

Decide how asserted, continuing, effective-dated, immutable, derived, corrected, superseded, withdrawn, and restricted Relationships are stored and resolved.

## ADR-HT-005 — Backlink and Reverse-Dependency Indexing

Decide how inbound discovery is generated, refreshed, authorized, audited, and protected against inference leakage.

## ADR-HT-006 — Trail Step Identity and Branching

Resolve whether Trail Step identity survives revisions and how alternatives and branches are represented without creating a general workflow engine.

## ADR-HT-007 — Contextual Authority and Epistemic Status

Decide the representation, validation, and display of authority scope, epistemic status, confidence, disagreement, and adoption.

## ADR-HT-008 — Completeness and Inference-Risk Policy

Decide how incomplete, restricted, stale, unavailable, and unresolved evidence affects completeness claims and safe disclosure.

## ADR-HT-009 — Deterministic Resolution and Publication Freeze

Decide how Live, Approval-Controlled, Pinned, and Historical-As-Of selections resolve and how publication freezes exact dependencies.

## ADR-HT-010 — Governed History Propagation

Decide how withdrawal, redaction, deletion, anonymization, erasure, and retention expiry affect references, projections, caches, publications, exports, and audit residue.

## ADR-HT-011 — Automated Contribution Provenance

Decide how agent identity, delegated authority, instruction references, material inputs, model or tool versions, limitations, and review requirements are represented.

## ADR-HT-012 — Portability and Independent Reconstruction

Decide export envelopes, documented semantics, permitted content inclusion, reference-only export, validation, and reconstruction tests.

## ADR-HT-013 — Constitutional Conformance Automation

Decide the machine-readable traceability format, repository validation rules, evidence freshness policy, and exception lifecycle.

## Recommended Order

1. ADR-HT-001 through ADR-HT-005 establish the graph and reference foundation.
2. ADR-HT-006 through ADR-HT-009 establish the first Trail and publication slice.
3. ADR-HT-011 and ADR-HT-013 establish automated contribution and documentation governance.
4. ADR-HT-010 and ADR-HT-012 follow pilot-specific policy and export research.
