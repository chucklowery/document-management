# Relationship and View Principles

These principles are normative for all downstream design.

## Relationships Are Authored Assertions

Every consequential Relationship shall preserve identity, origin, author or originating agent, intended meaning, context, authority, endpoints, applicable Revisions or selection policy, recorded time, and lifecycle state. Relationships may be reviewed, disputed, superseded, deprecated, withdrawn, or restricted without erasing history.

## Bidirectional Discovery Is Fundamental

Every managed Relationship shall be discoverable from source and target perspectives, subject to authorization and privacy constraints. Backlinks, citations, inclusions, derivations, contradictions, and downstream uses are required capabilities.

## Canonical Source Does Not Imply Canonical View

An authoritative source may support multiple valid views, assemblies, Trails, summaries, projections, and interpretations. No hierarchy, narrative, dashboard, or generated answer is the sole valid representation.

## Multiple Reading Paths Are First-Class

The system shall support authored Trails through Resources, Revisions, Relationships, records, and projections. Trails retain author, ordering, rationale, applicable versions, and history.

## Derivatives Shall Not Impersonate Sources

Assemblies, summaries, projections, generated outputs, rendered outputs, recommendations, and automated answers shall remain visibly distinguishable from authoritative sources. Reuse does not transfer authorship, ownership, authority, or interpretive control.
