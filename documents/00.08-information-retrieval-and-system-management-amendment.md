# Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management Amendment

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System  
**Status:** Ratified  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-27  
**Ratification basis:** Direct instruction to apply and commit the amendment to `main`

## 1. Purpose

This amendment establishes constitutional principles for information retrieval, dynamic input and output management, change propagation, concurrency, operational reliability, and the governance of derived access structures.

The existing Constitution and ratified amendments define what managed information means, how authority and history are preserved, and how Resources are related. This amendment adds the behavior required for people and automated agents to find, receive, change, evaluate, and deliver that information as the managed corpus changes from day to day.

## 2. Scope

This amendment governs:

- query and retrieval behavior;
- searchable-corpus and index lifecycle;
- ranking and relevance evaluation;
- relevance feedback;
- external and internal input contracts;
- change-event processing;
- synchronization, replay, backfill, and reconciliation;
- output generation, subscription, and delivery;
- concurrent contribution handling;
- derived-store authorization;
- operational service levels, recovery, and degraded operation;
- user-visible freshness, completeness, and change awareness.

It does not require one search engine, message broker, database, ranking algorithm, user interface, deployment topology, or vendor.

## 3. Constitutional Principles

### SM-P-001 — Retrieval Effectiveness Is Product Behavior

The ability to find information that is relevant to a user's authorized task is observable product behavior, not merely an implementation detail.

Retrieval requirements shall identify the searchable corpus, searchable unit, applicable authorization context, temporal boundary, and ranking or ordering policy where those distinctions affect meaning.

### SM-P-002 — Derived Access Structures Are Versioned and Explainable

Indexes, caches, facets, embeddings, materialized views, reverse-dependency structures, and ranking outputs are derived representations.

A consequential retrieval or discovery result shall identify enough information to explain its source corpus, material source Revisions or temporal boundary, index or projection state, ranking-policy Revision, generated time, and known limitations.

### SM-P-003 — Change Propagation Is Explicit and Recoverable

A durable change that affects retrieval, dependencies, Projections, subscriptions, or delivered outputs shall enter an explicit propagation process.

The system shall preserve enough event identity, causation, ordering scope, processing state, and retry history to prevent silent loss, support idempotent handling, expose delay, and permit authorized replay or reconstruction.

### SM-P-004 — Inputs Preserve Authority and Contract

Every managed input channel shall operate through a versioned contract that preserves source identity, authority designation, validation outcome, temporal information, idempotency information, provenance, and applicable governance.

Invalid or unprocessable input shall be quarantined or rejected visibly rather than silently discarded or partially accepted without an accountable record.

### SM-P-005 — Outputs Preserve Reproducibility and Delivery State

A material output shall remain distinguishable from its source and shall preserve an Output Manifest sufficient to explain or reproduce the output when policy requires.

Delivery attempts, acknowledgements, failures, retries, expirations, supersessions, and withdrawals shall be explicit and auditable.

### SM-P-006 — Relevance Feedback Is Governed Evidence

A user's relevance judgment, result selection, dismissal, or other permitted feedback is evidence about a task and retrieval interaction. It is not universal proof of truth, authority, popularity, or organizational value.

Feedback that changes a governed ranking policy shall be adopted through an explicit, reviewable policy and shall preserve evaluation evidence and affected scope.

### SM-P-007 — Current and Historical Views Coexist

Current retrieval, historical as-of retrieval, and scenario-based retrieval are distinct views.

A current index or ranking shall not make historical information unresolvable, and a historical query shall not include later information merely because it is present in the current corpus.

### SM-P-008 — Concurrent Contributions Are Reconciled Explicitly

When concurrent human or automated contributions are based on incompatible prior states, the system shall preserve acknowledged contributions, detect the conflict, and require an explicit reconciliation or supersession outcome.

Automatic merging is permitted only under a documented rule that preserves identity, provenance, and the semantic meaning of the affected fields and Relationships.

### SM-P-009 — Operational Commitments Are Measurable

Availability, processing delay, indexing delay, delivery delay, capacity, backup, restore, recovery, and reconstruction commitments shall be expressed through measurable service objectives or explicit pilot limits.

Operational status shall distinguish measured, estimated, projected, and unknown values.

### SM-P-010 — Authorization Applies to Every Derived Representation

Authorization, sensitivity, consent, retention, deletion, completeness-disclosure, and inference-risk policy shall apply to indexes, caches, facets, embeddings, counts, timing behavior, logs, explanations, exports, and delivered outputs—not only to authoritative source storage.

A policy change or revocation shall propagate to affected derived representations within an explicit policy deadline.

### SM-P-011 — Users Can Observe Change, Freshness, and Incompleteness

An authorized user shall be able to determine whether relevant information changed, how fresh a result or Projection is, whether processing is delayed or degraded, and whether material information is unavailable, restricted, stale, excluded, or unresolved.

The system shall not imply completeness or currency merely because a result was returned successfully.

### SM-P-012 — Replaceability Includes Retrieval and Delivery Infrastructure

Retrieval, indexing, event processing, connector, cache, and delivery implementations shall be replaceable without redefining durable domain identity, authority, provenance, or historical meaning.

Documented contracts and reconstruction procedures shall permit an authorized replacement implementation to rebuild derived structures from permitted canonical sources and durable processing records.

## 4. Required Operationalization

These principles shall be operationalized through:

- canonical terms and domain models;
- explicit bounded-context ownership;
- user roles and stories;
- EARS requirements;
- Given/When/Then acceptance specifications;
- architecture decisions;
- relevance and recovery evaluation evidence;
- constitutional coverage records.

## 6. Relationship to Existing Principles

This amendment extends, and does not replace:

- stable identity;
- immutable history;
- explicit authority;
- bounded-context ownership;
- source/derivative distinction;
- omission-aware provenance;
- human comprehension;
- attention governance;
- governed history;
- portability;
- proportional governance;
- empirical incremental delivery.

Where freshness conflicts with reproducibility, both states shall remain representable. Where ranking conflicts with plural views, ranking shall be treated as one governed Projection. Where centralized retrieval pressures bounded-context autonomy, cross-context exchange shall occur through published contracts rather than shared private models.
