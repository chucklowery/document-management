# Hypertext EARS Requirements Extension

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document extends `09-requirements-ears.md` with requirements derived from the Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment.

## REQ-HT-001 — Bidirectional Relationship Discovery

**EARS:** When an authorized user inspects a managed Resource, the system shall permit discovery of applicable managed Relationships from both source and target perspectives.

**Stories:** US-036

**Contexts:** Shared Graph Foundation; all consuming contexts

## REQ-HT-002 — Authorization-Aware Backlinks

**EARS:** If a user is not authorized to know that an inbound Relationship or target exists, then the system shall not disclose it through backlinks, counts, identifiers, ordering gaps, or errors.

**Stories:** US-036, US-044

**Contexts:** Shared Graph Foundation; Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-003 — Explicit Source Adoption Mode

**EARS:** When a destination references reusable source content, the system shall record whether selection is Pinned, Live, Approval-Controlled, or Historical-As-Of.

**Stories:** US-037

**Contexts:** Document Authoring and Composition

## REQ-HT-004 — Approval-Controlled Adoption

**EARS:** When an Approval-Controlled source has a newer applicable Revision, the system shall preserve the currently adopted Revision until an authorized Party records adoption of the proposed Revision.

**Stories:** US-037

**Contexts:** Document Authoring and Composition; Governance

## REQ-HT-005 — Distinguish Derivative Representation

**EARS:** When the system presents adapted, annotated, summarized, projected, rendered, or generated material, it shall identify the representation as derivative and preserve reference to its material sources.

**Stories:** US-038

**Contexts:** Document Authoring; Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-006 — Create Trail Revision

**EARS:** When a Trail Author records a Trail, the system shall create an immutable Trail Revision containing audience, purpose, ordered steps, target references, selection modes, rationale, and known limitations.

**Stories:** US-039

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-007 — Material Trail Change

**EARS:** When Trail purpose, audience, ordering, source selection, annotation, alternatives, or stated omissions materially change, the system shall create a new Trail Revision.

**Stories:** US-039

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-008 — Alternative Trails

**EARS:** The system shall permit multiple Trails and Trail Revisions to present different orderings, selections, annotations, or interpretations over the same source objects.

**Stories:** US-040

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-009 — Trail Comparison

**EARS:** When an authorized user compares Trails, the system shall identify differences in source selection, exact Revisions, ordering, annotations, alternatives, and stated omissions.

**Stories:** US-040

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-010 — Trail Adoption

**EARS:** When an authorized Party adopts a Trail, the system shall record the exact Trail Revision, adopting scope, authority, purpose, effective time, conditions, and recorded time.

**Stories:** US-041

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance; Governance

## REQ-HT-011 — Adoption Does Not Establish Universal Truth

**EARS:** When a Trail is adopted for a defined scope, the system shall not represent the Trail as universally authoritative outside that scope.

**Stories:** US-041

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-012 — Omission-Aware Provenance

**EARS:** When a synthesis, Trail, Projection, or generated answer is finalized, the system shall permit provenance to identify material sources, transformations, selection rules, applicable versions, exclusions, unavailable information, and limitations.

**Stories:** US-042, US-044

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance; analytical contexts

## REQ-HT-013 — No Unjustified Completeness Claim

**EARS:** If relevant information is materially unavailable, restricted, excluded, stale, or unresolved, then the system shall not present a synthesis, Trail, graph, Projection, or answer as complete.

**Stories:** US-042, US-044

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance; Governance

## REQ-HT-014 — Safe Completeness Disclosure

**EARS:** When completeness is uncertain, the system shall express uncertainty according to authorization and inference-risk policy without disclosing restricted information unsafely.

**Stories:** US-044

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-015 — Governed Historical Action

**EARS:** When an authorized historical withdrawal, redaction, access revocation, retention expiry, anonymization, deletion, or erasure action occurs, the system shall record the action, authority, policy, scope, effective time, and permitted accountability residue, if any.

**Stories:** US-043

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance; owning context

## REQ-HT-016 — References to Unavailable Content

**EARS:** When governed history makes referenced content unavailable, the system shall preserve or remove identifying reference residue according to policy and shall not fabricate replacement content.

**Stories:** US-043

**Contexts:** Shared Graph Foundation; Governance

## REQ-HT-017 — Independent Export

**EARS:** When an authorized portability export is requested, the system shall export permitted identities, Revision history, Relationships, Trails, provenance, manifests, annotations, and governance metadata in documented, independently interpretable forms.

**Stories:** US-045

**Contexts:** Integration and External Systems; Shared Graph Foundation

## REQ-HT-018 — Export Respects Governance

**EARS:** If information is restricted by sensitivity, consent, retention, legal, or deletion policy, then the system shall enforce that policy during portability export.

**Stories:** US-045

**Contexts:** Integration and External Systems; Governance

## REQ-HT-019 — Automated Contribution Provenance

**EARS:** When an Automated Agent creates a consequential contribution, the system shall record agent identity, delegated authority, instruction or policy reference and version, purpose, material constraints, material inputs, relevant tool or model version, limitations, and produced provenance.

**Stories:** US-046

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-020 — Restricted Instruction Disclosure

**EARS:** If full governing instructions contain sensitive, proprietary, personal, or security-relevant information, then the system shall restrict their disclosure while preserving an auditable instruction or policy reference.

**Stories:** US-046

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-021 — Explain Consequential Change

**EARS:** When a consequential change is finalized, the system shall permit intent, rationale, affected assumptions, expected impact, authority, dependencies, source evidence, and superseded scope to be recorded.

**Stories:** US-047

**Contexts:** All consequential domain contexts

## REQ-HT-022 — Attention Policy

**EARS:** Where notifications, dependency alerts, adoption requests, approvals, escalation, or automated recommendations are enabled, the system shall apply an explicit attention policy controlling audience, urgency, frequency, grouping, suppression, and acknowledgement.

**Stories:** US-048

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-023 — Constitutional Coverage

**EARS:** The project shall trace each normative constitutional principle to policy, User Stories, Requirements, Acceptance Specifications, verification evidence, or an approved time-bounded exception.

**Stories:** US-036 through US-048

**Contexts:** Documentation and governance

## Initial Priority

Priority for the first implementation increment:

- REQ-HT-001 through REQ-HT-005;
- REQ-HT-006 through REQ-HT-014;
- REQ-HT-019 through REQ-HT-021;
- REQ-HT-023.

Governed erasure, portability export, and attention policy require pilot-specific legal, operational, and product constraints before full implementation.
