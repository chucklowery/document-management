# Constitutional Amendment: Context Boundaries and Incremental Delivery

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This amendment clarifies how the shared Resource Graph, bounded contexts, projections, scope, and system-health requirements are to be interpreted.

## 1. Shared Graph Is a Conceptual Foundation

Participation in the shared Resource Graph does not require:

- one physical datastore;
- one graph database;
- one global schema;
- one transactional boundary;
- one runtime object model; or
- direct reuse of another bounded context's internal entities.

The shared foundation standardizes stable identity, revision addressing, relationship semantics, immutable-record references, authority designation, provenance, and cross-context traceability.

Each bounded context may use its own internal representation provided that information crossing a context boundary is translated through an explicit contract.

## 2. Context Map Is an Evolving Hypothesis

The bounded contexts listed in the Project Constitution are the initial context-map hypothesis.

The project shall maintain an explicit context map, but boundaries may be revised through an Architectural Decision Record when operational learning demonstrates that a different boundary better preserves language, authority, lifecycle, invariants, or delivery independence.

A context change shall document:

- the problem with the existing boundary;
- the affected language and authority;
- information ownership before and after the change;
- required translations or migrations;
- effects on published contracts and historical reconstruction.

## 3. Projection Reproducibility

A Projection shall identify enough information to explain and reproduce its result, including as applicable:

- Projection Definition identity and version;
- source Resources, Revisions, Relationships, and immutable records;
- recorded-time boundary;
- effective-time boundary;
- filters, assumptions, policies, and parameters;
- calculation or classification rules;
- generation time;
- uncertainty or confidence where relevant.

A Projection is derived information. It shall not silently replace the facts from which it was calculated.

## 4. Scope Horizons

Constitutional scope shall be interpreted through four delivery horizons.

### Foundation

Capabilities required by every initial vertical slice:

- stable identity;
- immutable Revisions and records;
- typed Relationships;
- provenance;
- authority designation;
- authorization;
- historical reconstruction;
- reproducible projections.

### Initial Vertical Slices

Thin, end-to-end workflows selected to validate the domain and architecture with users.

### Planned Capabilities

Capabilities expected to be delivered after the foundation and initial slices have been validated.

### Exploratory Capabilities

Capabilities whose domain model, value, or integration approach remains provisional and must be justified through demonstrated workflows, explicit risk, or empirical evidence.

Placement in constitutional scope does not imply simultaneous implementation.

## 5. Observability Principle

Important workflows, authority boundaries, governance controls, and derivations shall be observable.

The exact operational measure catalog—including stale references, pending approvals, reproducibility, coverage, delays, bypass, or provenance completeness—belongs in requirements and operational-governance documentation and may evolve without constitutional amendment.

## 6. Canonical Organizational Learning Loop

The canonical traceability loop is:

> **Evidence → Interpretation → Decision → Plan → Execution → Measurement → Outcome → New Evidence**

The phrases Sense, Learn, and Adapt remain explanatory language rather than additional mandatory lifecycle entities.

## 7. Effective Date

This amendment takes effect upon acceptance into the project repository and governs interpretation of the Project Constitution and subsequent domain models.
