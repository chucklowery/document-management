# Hypertext Domain Model Integration

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

This document extends `02-domain-model.md` and connects the Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment to the principal domain model.

## 1. Resource Kinds

The principal Resource catalog shall include:

- Document
- Artifact
- Knowledge Record
- Intent and Specification Record
- Work Resource
- Outcome Resource
- Trail

Trail is a first-class versioned Resource. It is not a projection, folder, workflow, or document subtype.

## 2. Relationship Contract Extension

A consequential Relationship contract shall define, as applicable:

- stable Relationship identity;
- source and target identity;
- Relationship Type;
- author or originating agent;
- originating context;
- intended meaning;
- exact Revision references or selection rule;
- recorded and effective time;
- authority or policy basis;
- provenance;
- correction, supersession, withdrawal, and restriction behavior;
- authorization-aware source and target discovery behavior.

Relationship lifecycle is owned by the relevant contract. Immutable asserted Relationships, effective-dated Relationships, continuing governed Relationships, and derived Relationships may use different lifecycle rules.

## 3. Trail Aggregate

The Trail aggregate is defined in `03.08-trail-domain-model.md` and contains:

- Trail Resource;
- immutable Trail Revision;
- ordered Trail Steps;
- selection modes;
- rationale and known limitations;
- review and adoption records;
- rendering and comparison projections.

The Knowledge and Provenance context owns Trail meaning and lifecycle. The Shared Graph Foundation supplies identity, Revision, and Relationship conventions.

## 4. Epistemic Contract

Consequential knowledge records shall permit explicit Epistemic Status and shall distinguish at minimum:

- Observation;
- report;
- inference;
- hypothesis;
- Finding;
- Recommendation;
- Decision;
- Projection;
- generated output;
- Verification Result;
- Intended Outcome;
- Observed Outcome.

Epistemic Status shall remain distinct from confidence, approval, authority, popularity, and adoption.

## 5. Contextual Authority

Authority shall be represented with explicit scope. Minimum applicable dimensions include:

- purpose;
- bounded context;
- organization or jurisdiction;
- decision scope;
- effective period;
- governing policy or delegation;
- responsible Party.

An adoption, approval, or publication record shall not silently universalize authority beyond its scope.

## 6. Derivative Representation Contract

A Derivative Representation shall preserve:

- representation identity and type;
- material sources and exact Revisions;
- generating Party or agent;
- transformation or assembly method;
- selection and ordering rules;
- material omissions and unavailable information;
- parameters and assumptions;
- tool or model versions where material;
- completeness limitations;
- creation time and applicable policy.

Derivative representations shall remain distinguishable from authoritative source.

## 7. Governed History Contract

A Governed History Action is an Immutable Record containing, where policy permits:

- action type;
- authority;
- governing policy;
- affected scope;
- effective and recorded time;
- required propagation behavior;
- permitted Accountability Residue;
- resulting reference behavior.

No universal residue requirement applies. Some policies may require complete removal of identifying linkage.

## 8. Automation Contribution Contract

A consequential automated contribution shall preserve:

- agent identity;
- Automation Delegation;
- purpose and scope;
- governing instruction or policy reference and version;
- material constraints and inputs;
- relevant tool or model version;
- limitations and uncertainty;
- produced provenance;
- required review or adoption authority.

## 9. Principal Invariants

1. A Trail is distinct from every object it references.
2. Trail ordering does not alter source order, ownership, authority, or meaning.
3. A Relationship does not establish truth merely by existing.
4. Backlink discovery does not transfer mutation authority.
5. A canonical source may support multiple valid views.
6. A Derivative Representation shall not impersonate source.
7. Material omission is part of provenance.
8. Restricted information shall not leak through identifiers, counts, gaps, errors, or graph topology beyond policy.
9. Governed history may make previously referenced content unavailable without fabricating replacement content.
10. The Shared Graph shall not own context-specific meaning or lifecycle.
11. Automated Agents shall not silently redefine terms, authority, disagreement, or epistemic status.
12. Uncovered constitutional principles are unimplemented, not satisfied.

## 10. Required Architecture Decisions

The domain model cannot be implemented completely until ADRs define:

1. identity formats and durability guarantees;
2. Content Region anchoring;
3. Relationship persistence, revision, and derivation;
4. backlink indexing and authorization filtering;
5. Trail Step identity and branch representation;
6. contextual authority representation;
7. completeness and inference-risk policy;
8. governed-history propagation;
9. canonical semantic serialization;
10. portability and independent reconstruction.
