# Findability and Information Architecture Domain Model

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Managed Concepts

- **Vocabulary** owns contextual terms, labels, aliases, definitions, mappings, and Revisions.
- **Navigation Model** defines permitted paths among Resources, Revisions, Relationships, Trails, Queries, and views.
- **Findability Session** records a task, entry point, Refinement State, navigation steps, selections, and outcome when collection is permitted.
- **Saved Workspace** preserves a task context, selected identities, Refinement State, view choices, and review checkpoint.
- **Personalization Policy** governs adaptive ordering, defaults, explanation, reset, and retention.
- **Management Change Plan** defines the scope and recovery behavior of a configuration or bulk change.
- **Impact Preview** and **Dry Run Result** are derived records and do not establish actual effects.

## Invariants

1. Labels and aliases do not replace stable identity.
2. Vocabulary ownership remains contextual.
3. Navigation paths reference managed identities rather than copied display text alone.
4. Refinement State identifies corpus, time boundary, and applied constraints.
5. Result previews remain derivative and traceable.
6. Personalization cannot change authority or mandatory governance information.
7. Saved workspaces preserve exact identities or explicit selection rules.
8. Administrative execution records the Management Change Plan Revision used.
9. Dry runs are distinguishable from execution.
10. Completed bulk changes preserve per-item outcomes and recovery status.
