# Processing and Capacity EARS Requirements

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## REQ-LO-005 — Declared Processing Boundary

**EARS:** Before a batch, stream, synchronization, replay, backfill, or delivery operation begins, the system shall identify the Processing Boundary and its atomicity, ordering, acknowledgement, checkpoint, retry, compensation, and partial-success rules.

**Stories:** US-093, US-094

## REQ-LO-006 — Item-Level Batch Outcome

**EARS:** When a bounded operation completes or stops, the system shall record summary state and item-level success, failure, skip, quarantine, retry, compensation, and unresolved state for consequential items.

**Stories:** US-093

## REQ-LO-007 — Resumable Checkpoint

**EARS:** When restartable processing advances a checkpoint, the system shall record the source position, completed effects, pending work, contract and processing definition Revisions, integrity, and acknowledgement rule required for authorized resume.

**Stories:** US-094

## REQ-LO-008 — Idempotency Retention Visibility

**EARS:** When duplicate-prevention evidence expires before duplicate risk ends, the system shall expose the applicable Idempotency Retention Window and require an explicit replay, reconciliation, or exception policy.

**Stories:** US-094

## REQ-LO-009 — Observable Backpressure

**EARS:** When arrival or delivery demand approaches or exceeds safe capacity, the system shall expose authorized backlog size, oldest work age, arrival and processing rates, capacity, Backpressure State, and effects on freshness or delivery objectives.

**Stories:** US-095

## REQ-LO-010 — Governed Admission and Load Shedding

**EARS:** While Backpressure State is active, the system shall apply an identified Admission Policy for acceptance, delay, priority, throttling, expiration, or rejection and shall record each consequential disposition without silently dropping acknowledged work.

**Stories:** US-095
