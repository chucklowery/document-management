# Counterpoint Article: The Narrative Is One Path

**Status:** Interpretive  
**Counterpoint:** Ted Nelson  
**Associated principles:** LO-P-001, LO-P-007  
**Associated requirements:** REQ-LO-013, REQ-LO-015, REQ-LO-016, REQ-LO-017

A literate operational narrative gives readers a deliberate sequence through complex behavior. That sequence is valuable, but it is not the only legitimate structure of the knowledge.

A Nelson-oriented counterpoint is that links, backlinks, transclusion, parallel versions, and alternative paths should remain first-class. A linear explanation can hide disagreement, alternate recovery paths, or the fact that one contract and one event participate in several workflows.

The system should treat an Operational Narrative as an authored Trail through exact sources and records. Readers should be able to inspect connected records, compare alternatives, review prior Revisions, and enter the operational graph from other valid paths without losing source identity.
