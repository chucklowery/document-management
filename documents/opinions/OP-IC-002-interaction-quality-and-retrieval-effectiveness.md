# Opinion Article OP-IC-002 — Interaction Quality and Retrieval Effectiveness

**Status:** Interpretive  
**Counterpoint:** Gerard Salton  
**Associated principles:** IC-P-001, IC-P-004, IC-P-005, IC-P-007  
**Associated requirements:** REQ-IC-001, REQ-IC-003, REQ-IC-006, REQ-IC-007, REQ-IC-009, REQ-IC-021

A clear control surface can help a user express an information need, but ease of use does not establish that retrieval or routing behavior is effective.

A Salton-oriented counterpoint is that configurable filters, mappings, ranking choices, and examples still require evaluation against representative queries and judgments.

The project should keep interaction evidence and retrieval evidence distinct. User control improves formulation and inspection; precision, recall, ranking quality, coverage, freshness, and latency still require explicit measurement.
