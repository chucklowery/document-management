# Counterpoint Article: Narrative and Measurement

**Status:** Interpretive  
**Counterpoint:** Gerard Salton  
**Associated principles:** LO-P-001, LO-P-007  
**Associated requirements:** REQ-LO-013, REQ-LO-015, REQ-LO-016, REQ-LO-017, REQ-LO-019

A Knuth-inspired operational narrative improves comprehension by arranging purpose, invariants, state changes, failures, and recovery in the order a person needs to reason about them.

A Salton-oriented counterpoint is that a clear account can still describe ineffective behavior. Quality must also be tested against representative information needs and observable measures. Retrieval effectiveness, latency, coverage, error rates, and contract conformance cannot be inferred from explanatory elegance.

The project should treat narrative and measurement as complementary evidence. Narratives explain what measures mean and why thresholds matter; evaluation results challenge the narrative when actual behavior differs. Neither persuasive prose nor a single metric is sufficient by itself.
