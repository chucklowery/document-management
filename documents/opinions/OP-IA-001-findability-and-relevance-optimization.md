# Opinion Article OP-IA-001 — Findability Is Broader Than Relevance Optimization

**Status:** Interpretive  
**Primary counterpoint:** Gerard Salton  
**Associated principles:** IA-P-001, IA-P-002, IA-P-012  
**Associated requirements:** REQ-IA-001, REQ-IA-002, REQ-IA-020, REQ-IR-013

## Position

Morville-oriented findability includes orientation, recognition, vocabulary, browsing, credibility, accessibility, and task completion in addition to ranked retrieval.

## Salton Counterpoint

An information-retrieval discipline requires measurable relevance behavior. Precision, recall, ranked evaluation, and representative queries prevent attractive navigation from concealing weak retrieval.

## Opinion

The system should retain rigorous retrieval evaluation while refusing to treat one relevance metric as the whole user experience. Search measures and task-based findability evidence are complementary and must be interpreted within corpus, audience, authorization, and task context.
