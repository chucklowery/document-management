# Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## 1. Purpose

This amendment incorporates a hypertext perspective into the Project Constitution without replacing the existing bounded-context, provenance, governance, or delivery principles.

Knowledge integrity requires preserving not only content, but also authored connections, alternative paths, contextual authority, disagreement, transformations, omissions, and the distinction between source and view.

## 2. Relationships Are Authored Assertions

A consequential Relationship is an authored or generated assertion with explicit semantics.

Its contract shall preserve, as applicable:

- stable identity;
- source and target identities;
- Relationship Type;
- author or originating agent;
- originating context;
- intended meaning;
- applicable exact Revisions or selection rule;
- recorded and effective time;
- authority or policy basis;
- provenance;
- correction, supersession, withdrawal, or restriction history.

Relationship lifecycle semantics shall be defined by the owning contract. Immutable asserted Relationships, effective-dated Relationships, governed continuing Relationships, and derived Relationships need not share one lifecycle model.

## 3. Bidirectional Discovery

Every managed Relationship shall be discoverable from source and target perspectives, subject to authorization, privacy, inference-risk, and retention policy.

The system shall support authorization-aware discovery of:

- backlinks;
- citations;
- inclusions and transclusions;
- derivations;
- contradictions;
- supersessions;
- downstream publications;
- downstream assemblies and projections;
- dependency and impact paths.

Discovery from both directions does not transfer ownership or mutation authority.

## 4. Canonical Source Does Not Imply Canonical View

An authoritative source may support multiple valid:

- views;
- assemblies;
- Trails;
- summaries;
- annotations;
- projections;
- interpretations;
- dashboards;
- generated answers.

No hierarchy, narrative, dashboard, Trail, or automated answer is the sole valid representation merely because it is prominent, published, popular, or centrally located.

## 5. Multiple Reading and Reasoning Paths

The system shall support authored Trails through Resources, Revisions, Relationships, Immutable Records, and Projections.

A Trail is a versioned Resource that records an ordered path for a stated audience and purpose. A rendered or navigable presentation of a Trail is a Projection and does not replace the Trail definition or its source objects.

Alternative Trails may coexist and may present different legitimate orderings, emphases, interpretations, or levels of detail.

## 6. Derivatives Shall Not Impersonate Sources

Assemblies, adaptations, summaries, annotations, Projections, generated outputs, rendered outputs, recommendations, and automated answers shall remain visibly distinguishable from authoritative source.

Reuse does not transfer:

- authorship;
- ownership;
- authority;
- consent;
- interpretive control;
- responsibility for source correction.

A destination owner is responsible for destination context, selection, adaptation, and adoption policy without becoming owner of the reused source.

## 7. Disagreement and Epistemic Status

Consensus shall not erase disagreement.

Competing interpretations, dissent, contradictory Findings, alternative plans, and unresolved disputes may coexist. Synthesis shall not silently remove or demote material perspectives.

Consequential claims shall distinguish applicable epistemic status, including:

- observation;
- report;
- inference;
- hypothesis;
- Finding;
- Recommendation;
- Decision;
- Projection;
- generated output;
- verification result;
- Intended Outcome;
- Observed Outcome.

Popularity, repetition, graph centrality, automation, and publication do not establish truth.

## 8. Omission-Aware Provenance

Material provenance shall identify, where applicable:

- sources and exact Revisions;
- contributors;
- transformations;
- selection and ordering rules;
- parameters and assumptions;
- material exclusions;
- unavailable, restricted, stale, or unresolved information;
- relevant tools or model versions.

A synthesis, Trail, Projection, or answer shall not claim completeness when its relevant evidence set is materially unavailable, restricted, excluded, stale, or unresolved.

The form of completeness disclosure shall follow authorization and inference-risk policy. The system shall not reveal the existence of restricted information when that disclosure would itself be unsafe or unauthorized.

## 9. Governed History

Historical integrity does not require universal permanent visibility or retention.

The system shall support governed:

- withdrawal;
- redaction;
- access revocation;
- consent revocation;
- retention expiry;
- legal deletion;
- identity anonymization;
- cryptographic erasure;
- Relationship withdrawal;
- preservation of permitted non-identifying aggregates.

Where lawful and safe, the system shall preserve the minimum accountability residue permitted or required by policy. Some policies may require that no identifying residue remain.

## 10. Human Comprehension and Attention

Structural completeness shall not be achieved at the expense of human comprehension.

Complexity shall be progressively disclosed. Consequential machine-readable structures and automated actions shall have usable human explanations.

Notifications, propagation notices, approval requests, dependency alerts, and automated recommendations consume attention and shall be governed to prevent unbounded interruption and alert fatigue.

## 11. Portability and Survivability

The system shall survive replacement of any particular implementation.

Content, identities, Revision history, Relationships, Trails, provenance, manifests, annotations, and governance metadata shall be exportable in documented, independently interpretable forms sufficient for authorized reconstruction.

Portability shall not bypass sensitivity, consent, retention, or legal restrictions.

## 12. Automation Governance

Automated Agents may propose, transform, summarize, analyze, or generate under explicit delegated authority.

They shall not silently:

- redefine terms;
- alter authority;
- resolve disagreement;
- adopt consequential updates;
- remove acknowledged work;
- convert inference into fact.

Automated contributions shall preserve:

- agent identity;
- delegated authority context;
- instruction or policy reference and version;
- purpose and material constraints;
- material inputs;
- relevant tool or model version;
- limitations and uncertainty;
- produced provenance.

Full governing instructions may be restricted when disclosure would expose sensitive, proprietary, or security-relevant information.

## 13. Shared Graph Boundary

The Shared Graph owns cross-context identity, addressability, and Relationship conventions.

Bounded contexts retain ownership of:

- language;
- meaning;
- invariants;
- lifecycle transitions;
- authoritative records;
- domain-specific Relationship semantics.

## 14. Explainable Consequential Change

A consequential change shall record, as applicable:

- intent;
- rationale;
- affected assumptions;
- expected impact;
- authority;
- relevant dependencies;
- source Evidence;
- superseded scope.

A content difference alone is not sufficient explanation.

## 15. Stewardship Cost

Information shall not be retained, linked, classified, propagated, or made authoritative without regard to:

- maintenance burden;
- sensitivity;
- staleness;
- expected value;
- responsible stewardship;
- retention policy;
- downstream dependency cost.

## 16. Constitutional Operationalization

Every normative constitutional principle shall trace to one or more of:

- domain policy;
- User Story;
- EARS Requirement;
- Acceptance Specification;
- verification result;
- approved time-bounded exception.

Uncovered principles shall be identified as unimplemented rather than assumed to be satisfied.
