# Findability and Information Architecture Amendment

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System  
**Status:** Ratified  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-27

## Purpose

This amendment applies a Peter Morville-oriented information-architecture perspective. It adds behavior for orientation, understandable labels, complementary discovery paths, recoverable exploration, accessible interaction, trustworthy presentation, daily change review, and safe system configuration.

It extends the Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management Amendment. Returning a result is insufficient unless users can understand where they are, why an item appears, what alternatives exist, what changed, and what actions are safe.

## Principles

### IA-P-001 — Task-Centered Findability

An authorized person shall be able to locate, recognize, understand, and act on information appropriate to a stated task. Successful query execution or high rank alone does not establish findability.

### IA-P-002 — Complementary Paths

Identity lookup, search, browse, facets, contextual links, backlinks, Relationships, Trails, chronology, and saved workspaces are complementary. No hierarchy or ranked list is the complete structure of the information space.

### IA-P-003 — Governed Contextual Vocabulary

Labels, preferred terms, aliases, definitions, and mappings shall identify their owning context and version when meaning is affected. Consequential vocabulary changes shall be impact-assessed and historically reconstructable.

### IA-P-004 — Orientation and Information Scent

Users shall be able to determine active scope, viewed identity and Revision, arrival path, related paths, and available actions. Labels and previews shall support informed navigation without replacing the source.

### IA-P-005 — Recoverable Exploration

Refinement, filtering, view changes, and navigation shall preserve visible state and support reversal. The system shall not silently broaden a failed query, discard filters, or change temporal or authorization scope to produce results.

### IA-P-006 — Credibility at the Decision Point

Consequential views shall expose authority, source or derivative status, applicable Revision or time boundary, freshness, uncertainty, limitations, and governance state.

### IA-P-007 — Transparent Personalization

Personalization shall identify material effects and permit reset or disablement where policy allows. It shall not alter authority, history, or mandatory governance information.

### IA-P-008 — Cross-Channel Continuity

Authorized movement among interactive views, saved workspaces, notifications, exports, and delivered outputs shall preserve or reconstruct identities, interaction state, filters, temporal boundary, and review checkpoint. Channel changes shall not silently change meaning.

### IA-P-009 — Reviewable Daily Change

Users shall be able to distinguish added, revised, superseded, withdrawn, newly relevant, no-longer-relevant, stale, failed, and unresolved information since a known checkpoint.

### IA-P-010 — Accessible Findability

Navigation, search, refinement, previews, status, errors, and management operations shall support assistive technology and non-pointer interaction. A path conveyed only visually or by color is insufficient.

### IA-P-011 — Previewable and Recoverable Management

Consequential changes to vocabularies, mappings, navigation, subscriptions, connectors, outputs, or many objects shall support impact preview or dry run when practical and record scope, effects, results, and rollback or compensation.

### IA-P-012 — Task-Based Evaluation

Findability shall be evaluated with representative tasks, audiences, authorization contexts, vocabularies, corpora, and change conditions. Measures shall include task success, time, abandonment, zero-result recovery, accessibility, credibility comprehension, and appropriate retrieval measures. No single metric establishes quality.
