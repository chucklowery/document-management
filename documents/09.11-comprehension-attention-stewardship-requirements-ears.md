# Comprehension, Attention, and Stewardship EARS Requirements

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document extends `09-requirements-ears.md` with requirements for human comprehension, attention governance, and stewardship cost.

## REQ-HT-024 — Human-Readable Consequential Explanation

**EARS:** When the system presents a consequential conclusion, status, recommendation, Projection, or automated answer, it shall provide a human-readable explanation of material sources, applicable authority, assumptions, uncertainty, and generated time.

**Stories:** US-049

**Contexts:** All presentation contexts

## REQ-HT-025 — Distinguish Epistemic Roles

**EARS:** When an explanation combines source facts, interpretations, Decisions, Projections, or generated material, the system shall identify their distinct epistemic roles.

**Stories:** US-049, US-051

**Contexts:** Knowledge and Provenance; all presentation contexts

## REQ-HT-026 — Progressive Disclosure

**EARS:** When presenting a complex Resource, Trail, Relationship graph, or explanation, the system shall provide a task-appropriate initial view and permit authorized users to inspect deeper provenance, Revisions, Relationships, policies, and calculation details.

**Stories:** US-050

**Contexts:** All presentation contexts

## REQ-HT-027 — Preserve Essential Risk Information

**EARS:** If uncertainty, restriction, authority scope, staleness, or material risk affects interpretation, then the system shall not hide that information solely to simplify presentation.

**Stories:** US-049, US-050

**Contexts:** All presentation contexts

## REQ-HT-028 — Human-Readable Derivative Comparison

**EARS:** When an authorized reviewer compares a Derivative Representation with its source, the system shall identify exact source Revisions and describe material transformations, omissions, annotations, and automated contributions in understandable terms.

**Stories:** US-051

**Contexts:** Document Authoring and Composition; Knowledge and Provenance

## REQ-HT-029 — Govern Notification Delivery

**EARS:** Where notifications are enabled, the system shall apply explicit audience, urgency, frequency, grouping, suppression, escalation, acknowledgement, and channel rules.

**Stories:** US-052

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-030 — Explain Notification Decision

**EARS:** When a notification is sent, grouped, suppressed, or escalated, the system shall record the triggering event, applicable attention policy, decision outcome, and time.

**Stories:** US-052, US-054

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-031 — Recipient Attention Preferences

**EARS:** When a recipient changes a permitted notification channel or non-mandatory frequency preference, the system shall apply the change prospectively while preserving mandatory policy-driven notices.

**Stories:** US-053

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-032 — Notification Explanation Privacy

**EARS:** If a notification explanation depends on restricted information, then the system shall provide only the explanation detail permitted by authorization and inference-risk policy.

**Stories:** US-054

**Contexts:** Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-033 — Stewardship Assignment

**EARS:** When a Resource is designated authoritative or operationally consequential, the system shall permit stewardship scope, responsible Party, effective period, and review policy to be recorded.

**Stories:** US-055

**Contexts:** All owning bounded contexts

## REQ-HT-034 — Staleness Projection

**EARS:** When a Resource, Relationship, Trail, policy, or Projection satisfies an explicit staleness rule, the system shall produce an explainable Staleness Projection without modifying the source object.

**Stories:** US-056

**Contexts:** Owning bounded context; Shared Graph Foundation

## REQ-HT-035 — Stewardship Review Outcome

**EARS:** When an authorized steward reviews a potentially stale item, the system shall record whether it was reaffirmed, revised, superseded, withdrawn, archived, consolidated, or retained with rationale.

**Stories:** US-056, US-058

**Contexts:** Owning bounded context; Governance

## REQ-HT-036 — Downstream Stewardship Assessment

**EARS:** When an authorized Source Owner requests a stewardship assessment, the system shall identify permitted inbound dependencies, review burden, sensitivity, propagation impact, and whether each cost indicator is measured, estimated, or policy-derived.

**Stories:** US-057

**Contexts:** Shared Graph Foundation; owning bounded context

## REQ-HT-037 — Stewardship Assessment Privacy

**EARS:** If downstream dependencies are restricted, then the system shall not disclose them through stewardship totals, identifiers, topology, or explanations beyond policy.

**Stories:** US-057

**Contexts:** Shared Graph Foundation; Governance

## REQ-HT-038 — Governed Information Retirement

**EARS:** When an authorized steward archives, withdraws, consolidates, or retires information, the system shall record rationale, authority, affected dependencies, retention policy, effective time, and resulting reference behavior.

**Stories:** US-058

**Contexts:** Owning bounded context; Identity, Access, and Governance

## REQ-HT-039 — Stewardship Cost Does Not Establish Truth

**EARS:** The system shall not treat stewardship cost, popularity, usage frequency, or dependency count as proof of truth, authority, or required retention.

**Stories:** US-057, US-058

**Contexts:** All contexts
