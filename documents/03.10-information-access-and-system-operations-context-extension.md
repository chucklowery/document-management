# Context Map Extension: Information Access, Retrieval, System Operations, and Delivery

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## 1. Purpose

This document extends `03-context-map.md` with bounded contexts required by the Information Retrieval and Dynamic System Management Amendment. It does not move authoritative domain meaning into retrieval or operations.

## 2. Information Access and Retrieval

**Purpose:** Convert authorized information needs into explainable, evaluable, current or historical retrieval views.

**Owns:** Query, Query Session, Corpus Definition, Searchable Unit contract, Index Revision semantics, Ranking Policy, Retrieval Projection, Relevance Explanation, Relevance Judgment, Retrieval Evaluation Set, Saved Search, and Change Digest.

**Consumes:** stable identity and Revision addressing; source content and metadata through owning-context contracts; authorization and completeness policy; relationship and backlink projections; and staleness and processing state.

**Does not own:** authoritative source content, domain lifecycle, decision authority, truth or epistemic authority, external authority, governance policy, or work, outcome, publication, and financial semantics.

## 3. System Operations and Delivery

**Purpose:** Make input processing, propagation, derived-state maintenance, output delivery, service objectives, degraded operation, and recovery observable and controllable.

**Owns:** processing-attempt records, Quarantine Records, processing checkpoints, Consistency Projections, Propagation Lag measurements, Output Manifests when no domain context owns them, Delivery Attempts, operational delivery state, Operational Service Objectives, Degraded Mode declarations, Recovery Plans, and Recovery Exercises.

**Consumes:** Change Events and source boundaries; connector contracts and synchronization records; authorization, attention, retention, and inference-risk policy; and output definitions from producing contexts.

**Does not own:** business meaning of a source change, domain authority, source correction, Decision, Plan, Measurement, Outcome, Publication, or Trail semantics, or recipient acceptance and adoption.

## 4. Customer–Supplier Relationships

1. Source-owning contexts supply authorized searchable representations through versioned contracts.
2. Information Access and Retrieval supplies Retrieval Projections, explanations, and evaluation evidence.
3. Integration and External Systems supplies validated synchronization boundaries and external authority mappings.
4. Source-owning contexts publish durable changes or reproducible change boundaries to System Operations and Delivery.
5. System Operations and Delivery supplies consistency, lag, failure, rebuild, and delivery-state Projections.
6. Identity, Access, and Governance supplies policy before indexing, retrieval, explanation, cache use, export, and delivery.
7. Producing contexts supply output definitions; operations records delivery without taking ownership of domain meaning.

## 5. Anti-Corruption Rules

1. Retrieval uses typed source references rather than copied local authority.
2. Ranking features use published semantics and do not infer equivalence from similarly named fields.
3. Connector payloads are translated before becoming managed information.
4. Operational status does not replace domain lifecycle.
5. Delivery acknowledgement does not become approval.
6. Retrieval feedback does not become authority or truth.
7. Indexes and caches are replaceable derived stores.
8. Operational telemetry does not reveal restricted source existence or topology.

## 6. Initial Walking Slice

```text
Imported Source Revision
→ validated canonical representation
→ durable Change Event
→ authorized retrieval indexing
→ Evidence query and result explanation
→ Finding and Decision provenance
→ later Source Revision
→ historical citation preservation
→ visible Change Digest
→ index and backlink propagation
→ output generation with manifest
→ subscription delivery
→ authorization revocation
→ derived-store removal verification
→ replay and reconstruction evidence
```
