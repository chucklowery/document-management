# Trail Domain Model

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the Trail contract introduced by the Hypertext Knowledge Integrity Amendment.

A Trail is a versioned Resource that expresses an authored, ordered reading or reasoning path through managed information for a stated audience and purpose.

A Trail does not create a new source of truth for the objects it references. It records an intentional path and context.

## 2. Owning Context

Trail meaning and lifecycle are owned by the Knowledge and Provenance context.

The Shared Graph provides identity, addressability, Revision, and Relationship conventions.

Document Authoring may provide editing and rendering capabilities without owning Trail semantics.

## 3. Aggregate

### 3.1 Trail

A continuing Resource with stable identity.

Minimum properties:

- Trail Identity;
- title;
- purpose;
- intended audience;
- owner or steward;
- visibility and sensitivity policy;
- applicable organizational or decision scope;
- current Revision reference;
- status Projection where useful.

### 3.2 Trail Revision

An immutable Resource Revision defining one historical Trail state.

Minimum properties:

- Trail Revision Identity;
- Trail Identity;
- author or originating agent;
- recorded time;
- rationale;
- ordered Trail Steps;
- applicable selection and ordering rules;
- stated omissions or known limitations;
- source Trail Revision when derived or revised;
- governing policy references.

### 3.3 Trail Step

An ordered component within a Trail Revision.

A Trail Step contains:

- stable identity within the Trail;
- ordinal or explicit ordering relation;
- target reference;
- target selection mode;
- optional annotation or rationale;
- optional transition explanation from the preceding step;
- optional expected reader action;
- optional visibility rule;
- optional alternative or branch designation.

A target reference may identify:

- Resource Identity with a selection rule;
- exact Resource Revision;
- exact Content Region occurrence;
- managed Relationship;
- Immutable Record;
- Projection Definition or historical Projection;
- another Trail or Trail Revision.

## 4. Selection Modes

A Trail Step shall use an explicit selection mode:

### 4.1 Pinned

References an exact immutable Revision, record, or historical Projection.

### 4.2 Live

Resolves the current applicable Revision according to an explicit policy.

### 4.3 Approval-Controlled

A newer applicable Revision is proposed but does not become part of the adopted Trail until an authorized Party records adoption.

### 4.4 Historical-As-Of

Resolves the object applicable at an explicit historical time or policy boundary.

## 5. Relationships

Minimum Relationship Types include:

- Trail Contains Step;
- Step References Target;
- Step Follows Step;
- Step Offers Alternative;
- Trail Derived From Trail;
- Trail Interprets Evidence;
- Trail Supports Decision;
- Trail Supersedes Trail Revision;
- Trail Published As Published Version.

The Relationship Type contract owns its lifecycle and authority semantics.

## 6. Invariants

1. Trail identity is distinct from the identity of every referenced object.
2. Trail ordering does not alter source ordering or source meaning.
3. A Trail Revision is immutable.
4. Material changes to purpose, audience, step ordering, target selection, annotation, or omissions create a new Trail Revision.
5. A Trail may reference exact Revisions or policy-selected current Revisions, but the mode shall be explicit.
6. A Trail may present one interpretation without claiming to be the only valid interpretation.
7. Alternative and contradictory Trails may coexist.
8. Trail authorship does not transfer authorship or ownership of referenced sources.
9. A rendered Trail is a Projection or generated representation and does not replace the Trail Revision.
10. Restricted targets shall not be disclosed through Trail metadata, ordering gaps, counts, or errors beyond policy.
11. A Trail shall not claim completeness when material sources were unavailable, restricted, excluded, stale, or unresolved.
12. Historical Trail Revisions remain reconstructable subject to governed history and retention policy.

## 7. Trail Status

Status is a Projection rather than a universal mutable field.

Possible projected states include:

- Draft;
- Under Review;
- Adopted;
- Superseded;
- Withdrawn;
- Incomplete;
- Stale;
- Restricted.

The Projection Definition identifies the policy and source records used to derive status.

## 8. Review and Adoption Records

Consequential review and adoption are represented by Immutable Records.

### 8.1 Trail Review

Records:

- exact Trail Revision;
- reviewer;
- authority or role;
- outcome;
- rationale;
- identified omissions or disputes;
- time.

### 8.2 Trail Adoption

Records:

- exact Trail Revision;
- adopting scope;
- adopting authority;
- adoption purpose;
- effective time;
- conditions;
- time.

Adoption does not establish universal truth or make the Trail authoritative outside its stated scope.

## 9. Rendering and Navigation

A Trail renderer may produce:

- linear reading view;
- branched view;
- evidence-to-decision view;
- decision-to-outcome view;
- comparison view;
- audience-specific summary;
- offline export.

Each rendered result shall identify:

- exact Trail Revision;
- resolution time;
- selected target Revisions;
- unresolved or restricted references where safe to disclose;
- rendering or Projection version.

## 10. Portability

An authorized Trail export shall include sufficient documented information to reconstruct:

- Trail identity and Revision history;
- ordered steps;
- target identities and selection modes;
- annotations and rationale;
- provenance;
- review and adoption records where authorized;
- unresolved references;
- applicable governance metadata.

Export does not imply permission to export the full content of every referenced target.

## 11. Initial Use Cases

1. Evidence-to-Decision reasoning path.
2. Decision-to-Project justification path.
3. Project-to-Outcome traceability path.
4. Publication reading order.
5. Alternative interpretation comparison.
6. Audit reconstruction path.
7. Onboarding or educational reading path.

## 12. Deferred Capabilities

- collaborative real-time Trail editing;
- automatic Trail recommendation;
- attention optimization;
- adaptive personalization;
- Trail popularity ranking;
- automatic dispute resolution;
- visual graph authoring frameworks.
