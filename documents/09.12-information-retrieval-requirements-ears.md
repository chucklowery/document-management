# Information Retrieval EARS Requirements

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document adds retrieval behavior without replacing existing requirements.

## REQ-IR-001 — Identified Retrieval Context

**EARS:** When a Party or Automated Agent submits a Query, the system shall evaluate it within an identified Corpus Definition, authorization context, temporal policy, and Ranking Policy or explicit non-ranked ordering policy.

**Stories:** US-059, US-061

## REQ-IR-002 — Identified Searchable Unit

**EARS:** When the system returns a Search Result, it shall identify the managed Resource, exact Revision or selection rule, and Content Region or record occurrence where the returned unit is narrower than the Resource.

**Stories:** US-059, US-060

## REQ-IR-003 — Index Accepted Change Within Objective

**EARS:** When an accepted source change is eligible for retrieval, the system shall make the permitted change retrievable within the applicable indexing Operational Service Objective or expose the delayed Consistency State.

**Stories:** US-059, US-068

## REQ-IR-004 — Separate Identity Lookup and Relevance Ranking

**EARS:** When a Query specifies an exact managed identity, the system shall resolve that identity according to authorization and historical policy without allowing relevance ranking to substitute a different object.

**Stories:** US-059, US-061

## REQ-IR-005 — Explain Ranked Inclusion and Order

**EARS:** When results are relevance-ranked, the system shall provide an authorized Relevance Explanation identifying the principal textual, metadata, relationship, authority, freshness, and policy factors that materially affected inclusion or order.

**Stories:** US-060

## REQ-IR-006 — Result Context and Freshness

**EARS:** When a Search Result is presented, the system shall identify its authority designation, applicable Revision or temporal boundary, generated time, freshness or staleness state, and derivative status.

**Stories:** US-060, US-075

## REQ-IR-007 — Multiple Valid Result Views

**EARS:** Where multiple ordering, grouping, Trail, graph, chronological, or authority-sensitive views are valid, the system shall permit the applicable view and policy to be identified and shall not represent one ranked list as the sole canonical structure.

**Stories:** US-059, US-060

## REQ-IR-008 — Historical As-Of Retrieval

**EARS:** When an authorized user requests retrieval as of a recorded-time or effective-time boundary, the system shall exclude information that was not applicable to that boundary and shall identify the temporal interpretation used.

**Stories:** US-061

## REQ-IR-009 — Saved Search Revision

**EARS:** When an owner saves or materially changes a governed Query, corpus, filters, temporal policy, or notification behavior, the system shall create or revise a Saved Search while preserving prior acknowledged Revisions.

**Stories:** US-062

## REQ-IR-010 — Change Digest from Checkpoint

**EARS:** When a user requests changes since a saved checkpoint or prior review, the system shall produce an authorized Change Digest that identifies added, revised, superseded, withdrawn, newly relevant, and no-longer-relevant results according to an explicit comparison policy.

**Stories:** US-062

## REQ-IR-011 — Contextual Relevance Judgment

**EARS:** When permitted relevance feedback is recorded, the system shall preserve the Query or task context, Searchable Unit, assessor, judgment, time, and applicable corpus and ranking-policy versions.

**Stories:** US-063

## REQ-IR-012 — Govern Feedback Adoption

**EARS:** When relevance feedback is used to change a governed Ranking Policy, the system shall require an authorized adoption record identifying scope, evaluation evidence, expected effect, affected users, and rollback condition.

**Stories:** US-063, US-064

## REQ-IR-013 — Retrieval Evaluation Baseline

**EARS:** When a material Ranking Policy Revision is proposed, the system shall evaluate it against a versioned Retrieval Evaluation Set using task-appropriate precision, recall, ranked-result, freshness, safety, and latency measures before adoption.

**Stories:** US-064

## REQ-IR-014 — Reject Material Ranking Regression

**EARS:** If a proposed Ranking Policy Revision causes an unapproved material Ranking Regression, then the system shall prevent adoption or require an explicit time-bounded exception with scope, rationale, authority, monitoring, and rollback criteria.

**Stories:** US-064

## REQ-IR-015 — Incomplete Retrieval Disclosure

**EARS:** If relevant information is materially unavailable, restricted, stale, excluded, unresolved, failed, or still processing, then the system shall not present the Retrieval Projection as complete and shall disclose permitted limitations without leaking restricted information.

**Stories:** US-075
