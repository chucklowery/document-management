# Information Retrieval and System Management Role Extension

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## 1. Purpose

This document defines contextual roles required to operate retrieval, inputs, outputs, propagation, evaluation, and recovery. A role grants no authority outside its recorded scope and effective period.

## 2. Roles

### Information Seeker

Submits Queries, refines results, inspects explanations, saves searches, and records permitted relevance feedback.

### Retrieval Steward

Owns Corpus Definitions, Ranking Policies, retrieval evaluation baselines, and ranking-change review for a defined scope. This role does not own authoritative source content.

### Relevance Assessor

Creates governed Relevance Judgments for identified tasks and evaluation sets. A judgment is contextual evidence, not organizational truth.

### Connector Steward

Owns an Input Contract or Output Contract, synchronization policy, mapping, checkpoint, retry, quarantine, and backfill behavior for a defined integration.

### Processing Operator

Inspects processing attempts, retries eligible work, manages quarantined records, initiates authorized replay, and records operational actions.

### Output Publisher

Authorizes generation of a material output and its Output Manifest within a defined scope.

### Subscription Owner

Creates and maintains an authorized Subscription and its recipient, frequency, channel, and acknowledgement preferences.

### Delivery Operator

Inspects delivery state, retries eligible deliveries, suppresses unsafe delivery, and escalates terminal failure according to policy.

### Reconciliation Authority

Resolves a defined class of concurrent-change conflicts or approves a Reconciliation Record.

### Operations Steward

Owns Operational Service Objectives, capacity limits, degraded-mode declarations, recovery plans, and recovery exercises for a defined capability.

### Derived-Store Security Steward

Approves and verifies authorization, revocation, retention, erasure, and leakage controls for indexes, caches, facets, embeddings, logs, and delivery stores.

## 3. Separation Rules

1. Retrieval stewardship does not grant source modification authority.
2. Connector stewardship does not transfer external or local source authority.
3. Processing operation does not grant permission to alter quarantined payload meaning.
4. Output publication does not grant recipient acceptance authority.
5. Relevance assessment does not grant ranking-policy adoption authority unless separately assigned.
6. Operations authority does not permit bypass of approval, consent, retention, or disclosure policy.
7. Reconciliation authority is scoped by context, Resource kind, Relationship type, or conflict class.
