# Literate Operations Canonical Terms

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

These terms describe contract evolution, recoverable high-volume processing, operational explanation, drift repair, and safe channel retirement without prescribing a vendor or deployment topology.

## Terms

### Channel Catalog Entry

A versioned record identifying an input, output, event, synchronization, or delivery channel; its owner; direction; authority; active contract Revisions; authentication; temporal policy; service objectives; retention; dependencies; and lifecycle state.

### Compatibility Classification

A governed assessment that a proposed contract change is backward compatible, forward compatible, conditionally compatible, breaking, or unknown for a stated producer and consumer boundary.

### Contract Migration Plan

A versioned plan identifying affected producers and consumers, source and target contract Revisions, transformations, dual-run or cutover behavior, validation, rollback or compensation, notice, checkpoints, and completion criteria.

### Canonical Envelope

A durable representation that preserves source identity, contract Revision, authority, integrity, temporal values, idempotency and ordering information, payload or protected payload reference, validation outcome, and mapping provenance.

### Processing Boundary

The declared unit within which atomicity, ordering, checkpoint, retry, acknowledgement, and partial-success rules apply.

### Batch Outcome

A durable summary and item-level record of success, failure, skip, quarantine, retry, compensation, and unresolved state for a bounded operation.

### Resume Checkpoint

A durable position and state boundary from which an interrupted operation can continue using identified contract, mapping, policy, and processing definition Revisions.

### Idempotency Retention Window

The period and scope for which the system retains enough identity and outcome information to recognize a repeated intended operation.

### Backpressure State

An explainable Projection describing queue depth or work inventory, arrival and processing rates, age, capacity, throttling, priority, expiration, rejection, and expected effect on freshness or delivery.

### Admission Policy

A versioned policy that determines whether work is accepted, delayed, prioritized, throttled, expired, or rejected when demand approaches or exceeds safe capacity.

### Drift Finding

A durable finding that identifies a material difference between authoritative state and an expected derived representation or delivery state, including comparison definition, boundaries, affected identities, severity, detected time, and disposition.

### Repair Run

A durable record of an authorized attempt to reconcile a Drift Finding, including plan, source boundary, definitions, affected objects, item-level outcomes, verification, and unresolved residue.

### Correlation Trail

A navigable set of stable correlation and causation identities connecting source intake, validation, mapping, domain effects, Change Events, processing attempts, derived representations, outputs, and deliveries.

### Operational Narrative

A versioned Resource written in a human-comprehensible order that explains an operation's purpose, authority, inputs, outputs, invariants, state transitions, failures, observability, recovery, and linked executable definitions.

### Narrative Conformance Result

A durable Verification Result showing whether identifiers, contracts, states, examples, and executable checks referenced by an Operational Narrative exist and agree within the evaluated boundary.

### Deprecation Notice

A versioned notice identifying the retiring capability or contract, affected scope, replacement or export path, notice dates, migration obligations, exceptions, and Sunset Boundary.

### Sunset Boundary

The effective boundary after which a deprecated contract, channel, connector, format, or processing definition no longer accepts or produces governed work except through an explicit exception.

### Clock Uncertainty

A recorded bound or status describing known uncertainty in a timestamp because of source precision, timezone ambiguity, synchronization error, delayed observation, or an untrusted clock.

## Usage Rules

1. A Channel Catalog Entry does not transfer source authority.
2. Compatibility is contextual to identified producers, consumers, and behavior.
3. A Resume Checkpoint is not proof that all prior effects completed unless the checkpoint contract states so.
4. Backpressure State distinguishes measured, estimated, projected, and unknown values.
5. A Repair Run does not erase the Drift Finding or prior incorrect derived state.
6. An Operational Narrative does not replace executable verification.
7. A Sunset Boundary does not authorize destruction that conflicts with retention or legal policy.
