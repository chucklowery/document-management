# Literate Operations Domain Model

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This model defines the durable records needed to evolve many input and output channels, process daily changes safely, explain operations, and reconstruct results.

## Channel and Contract Records

A **Channel Catalog Entry** identifies a channel, owner, direction, active contract Revisions, formats, time policy, service objectives, dependencies, consumers, and lifecycle state.

A **Compatibility Assessment** classifies a proposed contract change for named producer and consumer boundaries. Compatibility includes meaning and behavior, not only schema syntax. Unknown compatibility is not treated as compatible.

A **Contract Migration Plan** identifies source and target Revisions, affected parties, transformations, cutover or dual-run behavior, checkpoints, validation, recovery, notice, exceptions, and completion evidence.

A **Canonical Envelope** preserves source identity, contract Revision, integrity, temporal values, idempotency and ordering information, the received value or a governed reference, validation, and mapping provenance. Mapping does not erase received meaning.

## Processing Records

A **Processing Boundary** declares atomicity, ordering, acknowledgement, checkpoint, retry, compensation, and partial-success rules.

A **Batch Outcome** contains summary state and item-level success, failure, skip, quarantine, retry, compensation, and unresolved state. Summary success cannot conceal consequential item failure.

A **Resume Checkpoint** identifies source position, completed effects, pending work, and applicable definition Revisions. Checkpoint advancement follows the declared acknowledgement rule.

An **Idempotency Retention Window** defines how long duplicate-recognition evidence remains available. Expiration is visible when duplicate risk remains.

A **Backpressure State** reports work inventory, age, arrival and processing rates, capacity, priorities, expiration, rejection, and effects on freshness or delivery.

An **Admission Policy** governs acceptance, delay, priority, throttling, expiration, and rejection during overload. Acknowledged work is not silently dropped.

## Consistency and Repair Records

A **Drift Finding** identifies a material difference between an authoritative boundary and an expected derived representation, including comparison definition, affected identities, severity, time, and disposition.

A **Repair Run** identifies the plan, source boundary, definitions, checkpoints, item-level outcomes, verification, and unresolved residue. Repair does not rewrite authoritative history, and verification is distinct from the attempt.

## Literate Operations Records

An **Operational Narrative** is a versioned Resource organized for human understanding. It explains purpose, actors, inputs, outputs, invariants, state transitions, failures, observability, governance, recovery, and examples. It links to exact contracts, policies, executable checks, runbooks, and Verification Results.

A **Narrative Conformance Result** records the Narrative Revision, referenced definitions, evaluated boundary, checks, mismatches, time, and evidence. A missing or stale executable link is a visible failure.

## Lineage, Retirement, and Time

A **Correlation Trail** connects source receipt, validation, mapping, accepted Revisions, Change Events, Processing Attempts, Drift Findings, Repair Runs, Output Manifests, and Delivery Attempts.

A **Deprecation Notice** identifies affected channels, contracts, consumers, replacement or export path, notice and migration boundaries, exceptions, and Sunset Boundary. Retirement does not silently discard pending or acknowledged work.

Consequential records preserve applicable source, observation, effective, recorded, processing, acknowledgement, and delivery times, including timezone or offset, precision, and known clock uncertainty when material.

## Invariants

1. A channel has an accountable owner and explicit active contract Revisions.
2. A breaking change requires an approved migration or time-bounded exception.
3. Atomic and partial-acceptance modes are declared before execution.
4. Checkpoints and retries preserve identity and prior attempt history.
5. Overload does not weaken governing controls or silently lose acknowledged work.
6. Drift detection does not silently mutate either side.
7. Repair and repair verification remain distinct records.
8. An Operational Narrative does not replace executable verification.
9. A derived view, connector, narrative, or dashboard does not become authoritative merely by centralizing visibility.

## Ownership

Producing and consuming contexts own contract meaning. Integration and External Systems owns adaptation and migration records. System Operations and Delivery owns checkpoints, backpressure, consistency audits, repair, and delivery operations. The context owning an operation owns its Operational Narrative. Shared Graph Foundation supplies durable identity and relationship conventions.
