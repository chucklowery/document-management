# Human Comprehension User Stories

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Purpose

This document extends `08-user-stories.md` with dedicated stories for understandable explanations and progressive disclosure.

## US-049 — Understand a Consequential Conclusion

**As an affected reader,** I want a consequential conclusion, status, recommendation, or automated answer explained in understandable language, so that I can make an informed judgment.

**Owning contexts:** Presenting context; Shared Graph Foundation

### Acceptance examples

- The explanation identifies the conclusion, material sources, applicable authority, assumptions, uncertainty, and generated time.
- Technical identifiers and machine structures are available as deeper detail rather than required for basic comprehension.
- The explanation distinguishes source facts, interpretations, decisions, projections, and generated material.
- The explanation does not claim certainty or completeness beyond available evidence.

## US-050 — Progressively Disclose Complexity

**As a reader,** I want a simple initial view with access to deeper provenance and structural detail, so that I can understand the system without losing the ability to inspect it.

**Owning contexts:** All presentation contexts

### Acceptance examples

- The initial view presents the minimum information needed for the current task.
- The user can expand provenance, revisions, relationships, policies, and calculation details.
- Expansion preserves context and does not silently change the underlying conclusion.
- Essential risk, uncertainty, restriction, or authority information is not hidden solely to simplify presentation.

## US-051 — Compare Source and Derivative Meaning

**As a reviewer,** I want a human-readable comparison between a source and a derivative representation, so that I can understand transformations, omissions, annotations, and generated contributions.

**Owning contexts:** Document Authoring and Composition; Knowledge and Provenance

### Acceptance examples

- The comparison identifies exact source Revisions.
- Material transformations and omissions are described in understandable terms.
- Generated or automated contributions are visibly identified.
- The derivative remains distinguishable from the source.
