# Interactive Control Role Extension

**Project:** Organizational Knowledge and Work System

## Configuration Author

Prepares Configuration Drafts, examples, mappings, rules, previews, and proposed Activation Plans within granted authority.

The author cannot approve or activate behavior beyond assigned authority merely because the interface permits authoring it.

## Configuration Approver

Reviews proposed scope, contracts, validation, simulation evidence, impact, rollout, and recovery before granting required approval.

Approval applies to an exact Configuration Revision and Activation Plan boundary.

## Impact Reviewer

Examines affected identities, channels, users, policies, dependencies, unknowns, and predicted outcomes. The reviewer may require additional examples, reduced scope, or renewed preview.

## Operation Controller

Initiates, monitors, pauses, cancels, resumes, or stops an authorized execution and records the operational reason for intervention.

The controller does not represent completed external effects as undone by cancellation.

## Reversal Authority

Authorizes restoration, rollback, or Compensation Actions when ordinary operation cannot safely recover without consequential change.

## Override Authority

Approves a Manual Override with explicit scope, safeguards, expiration, monitoring, and review. Override authority is separate from ordinary configuration authorship unless policy explicitly combines them.

## Configuration Reviewer

Compares Configuration Revisions, examines history and outcomes, and verifies that active behavior still matches current contracts, policy, and user needs.

## Separation and Delegation Rules

1. High-consequence changes may require separation among author, approver, and operator.
2. Delegation identifies scope, duration, and permitted actions.
3. Automated agents may fill a role only through explicit policy and identity.
4. Emergency override does not silently become ordinary configuration.
5. Role decisions and exceptions are auditable.
