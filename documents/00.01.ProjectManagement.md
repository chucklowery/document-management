# Project Management 

A strong vision statement for this application should elevate it beyond a project management tool and position it as a comprehensive work intelligence and portfolio management platform.

### Vision Statement

> **To provide organizations with a unified system of record for planning, executing, measuring, and optimizing all work—from strategic initiatives to daily activities—connecting business outcomes, financial investments, resource utilization, and operational execution in a single, intelligent platform.**

### Expanded Vision

Organizations struggle to connect strategy, investment, execution, and outcomes across departments, programs, and projects. Work is often fragmented across spreadsheets, project management tools, time-tracking systems, financial systems, and reporting platforms, making it difficult to answer fundamental questions:

* What strategic initiatives are we investing in?
* How much are they costing?
* Where are resources spending their time?
* What outcomes are being delivered?
* Are we investing enough in innovation, maintenance, technical debt reduction, and operational support?
* How do efforts align with organizational goals?

This platform provides a hierarchical framework that organizes work across:

* Organizations
* Programs
* Projects
* Milestones
* Activities

while creating traceability between strategic objectives, operational execution, financial investment, and measurable outcomes.

### Core Principles

#### Strategic Alignment

Every activity should be traceable to a project, program, and organizational objective, ensuring that daily work contributes to measurable business outcomes.

#### Portfolio Visibility

Organizations should gain complete visibility into:

* Capitalized vs. operational work
* Billable vs. non-billable effort
* Technical debt initiatives
* Product development
* Infrastructure investments
* Project management overhead
* Unplanned and support work
* Compliance and governance efforts

#### Financial Intelligence

The platform should enable organizations to understand:

* Investment allocation
* Resource costs
* Labor utilization
* Capitalization opportunities
* Program ROI
* Cost per outcome
* Budget consumption
* Forecasted expenditures

#### Outcome-Based Management

Success should not be measured solely by tasks completed but by outcomes delivered, including:

* Revenue generated
* Costs reduced
* Risk mitigated
* Customer satisfaction improved
* Technical debt retired
* System reliability increased
* Strategic objectives achieved

### Organizational Model

```text
Organization
 ├── Program
 │    ├── Project
 │    │    ├── Roadmap
 │    │    ├── Milestones
 │    │    └── Activities
 │    └── Project
 │
 └── Program
      └── Projects
```

### Work Classification Framework

Programs and Projects should support configurable classifications such as:

| Category    | Examples                                 |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Financial   | Capitalized, Operational                 |
| Revenue     | Billable, Non-Billable                   |
| Strategic   | Growth, Innovation, Transformation       |
| Operational | Maintenance, Support, Unplanned Work     |
| Technology  | Technical Debt, Infrastructure, Security |
| Governance  | Compliance, Audit, Risk Management       |
| Product     | Features, Enhancements, Research         |
| Resource    | Internal, Contractor, Vendor             |

Organizations should be able to create custom classifications and reporting dimensions.

### Planning Capabilities

The platform should provide:

* Strategic roadmaps
* Program roadmaps
* Project roadmaps
* Milestone planning
* Dependency management
* Resource planning
* Capacity planning
* Budget planning
* Forecasting
* Scenario analysis

### Activity Intelligence

Activities represent the atomic unit of work and should support:

* Time tracking
* Estimated effort
* Actual effort
* Labor rates
* Cost calculations
* Billable rates
* Capitalization eligibility
* Priority
* Risk
* Outcome attribution
* Tags and classifications

Activities should be reusable and shareable across projects, programs, and organizations where appropriate.

### Reporting and Analytics

The platform should answer questions such as:

#### Portfolio Reporting

* What are we working on?
* How much effort is being invested?
* Which programs are over budget?
* Which projects are behind schedule?

#### Financial Reporting

* Capitalized vs operational spending
* Cost by organization
* Cost by program
* Cost by project
* Cost by outcome
* Billable utilization

#### Resource Reporting

* Team utilization
* Capacity forecasts
* Skill allocation
* Resource bottlenecks

#### Outcome Reporting

* Features delivered
* Technical debt retired
* Revenue enabled
* Customer impact
* Strategic goal attainment

### Future Vision

Ultimately, the platform becomes an **Enterprise Work Intelligence System** that not only records work but helps organizations make better investment decisions by answering:

> **Where should we invest our next dollar, hour, and resource to maximize organizational outcomes?**

By connecting strategy, planning, execution, financials, and outcomes into a single data model, the platform enables leaders to manage work as a portfolio of investments rather than a collection of disconnected projects and tasks.
