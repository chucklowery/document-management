# Requirements Document

## Introduction

This document specifies the requirements for the **fourth walking slice** of the Organizational Knowledge and Work System: the minimum end-to-end software capability that carries a Completion Record (produced by the [third walking slice](../third-walking-slice/requirements.md)) through to one or more recorded Measurement Records, an Observed Outcome, a Success-Condition Assessment, and an issued Outcome Review, without recording any Learning Record or adaptation Decision and without retroactively modifying any Slice 1, Slice 2, or Slice 3 row.

The slice realizes the pipeline:

```text
Completed Plan Revision ──Addresses (Slice 3 Completion)──> Completion Record
                                                                    │
                                                                    │ (Slice 4 entry)
                                                                    ▼
Intended Outcome Revision (Slice 2) ──Addresses──> Measurement Definition Revision
                                                          │
                                                          │ Produces / Cites
                                                          ▼
                                                  Measurement Record           (native or imported)
                                                          │
                                                          │ Cites (one or many)
                                                          ▼
                                                  Observed Outcome Revision    (outcome_kind = observed)
                                                          │
                                                          │ Addresses
                                                          ▼
                                                  Success-Condition Assessment (Immutable Record)
                                                          │
                                                          │ Cites
                                                          ▼
                                                  Outcome Review               (Governance Decision Immutable Record)
                                                          │
                                                          │ Addresses (Intended Outcome) + Cites (Completion, produced Deliverable Revision)
                                                          ▼
                                                  Slice 1 Decision  ←──  Recommendation  ←──  Finding  ←──  Content Region  ←──  Document Revision
```

and must demonstrate six named behaviors end-to-end:

1. **Completion-to-Outcome-Review traceability** — every Outcome Review is provably anchored to (a) an Intended Outcome Revision produced by the second walking slice and (b) a Completion Record produced by the third walking slice, through an unbroken chain Outcome Review → Success-Condition Assessment → Observed Outcome Revision → Measurement Record(s) → Measurement Definition Revision → Intended Outcome Revision → Objective → Slice 1 Decision → Recommendation → Finding(s) → Content Region Occurrence(s) → Document Revision, with a parallel chain Outcome Review → Cites Completion Record → produced Deliverable Revision(s).
2. **Intended/Observed separation enforced from the outcome side** — Slice 4 records observed-side facts only (Measurement Records, Observed Outcome Revisions, Success-Condition Assessments, Outcome Reviews); Slice 4 SHALL NOT mutate, supersede, or extend any Intended Outcome Resource or Revision, Deliverable Expectation Resource or Revision, or Plan Revision created by Slice 2, and SHALL NOT mutate any Completion Record, Milestone Acceptance Record, Deliverable Production Record, Work Assignment Record, Work Event Record, Time Entry Record, or produced Deliverable Revision created by Slice 3.
3. **Output is not Outcome (re-asserted from the outcome side)** — recording a Completion Record (Slice 3) does not by itself create or imply any Slice 4 record; an Outcome Review SHALL record an explicit attribution stance (asserted, partial, unattributed, contradicted) per Principle 5.21 and SHALL NOT be inferred from completion alone.
4. **Distinct define_measurement / record_measurement / assess_outcome / issue_outcome_review authorities** — four additive authority types extend the Slice 1 + Slice 2 + Slice 3 enumeration `{view, modify, review, approve, assign, contribute, accept_milestone, complete}` to twelve distinct authority types; no two of the new types are substituted for each other and none is substituted for any prior type.
5. **External authority preservation for imported Measurement Records** — every Measurement Record carries an origin indicator drawn from `{native, imported}`; an imported Measurement Record additionally carries the source-system identifier, the source-system record identifier, the source-system authority designation drawn from `{authoritative, replica, projection, index, federation}`, and the import time, per Principle 5.27 (External Authorities Remain Explicit).
6. **Indistinguishable denial for unauthorized outcome-measurement actions** — every denial path on the new Measurement, Observed Outcome, Success-Condition Assessment, and Outcome Review endpoints conforms to the `slice-default-2026` disclosure policy (AD-WS-9), additively extended to the new node kinds, with no information leakage about the existence, attributes, or counts of restricted outcome-measurement Resources or Records.

The slice is **executable system behavior**, distinct from documentation work. It is the software realization of *Release 1D — Deliverable to Outcome Review* defined in [`documents/07-user-story-map.md`](../../../documents/07-user-story-map.md) §4, constrained by the foundational system model in [`documents/00-project-constitution.md`](../../../documents/00-project-constitution.md) §5.21 (Intent/Work/Output/Outcome are distinct), §5.22 (Organizational Learning Is a Closed Loop), §5.23 (Operational Events and Current Projections Are Distinct), §5.27 (External Authorities Remain Explicit), and §5.30 (System Health Must Be Observable), by the Outcome Measurement and Learning context defined in [`documents/03-context-map.md`](../../../documents/03-context-map.md) §2.8, and by the Immutable Record contracts in [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §§7.4, 8.4, 8.5.

These requirements reconcile with, and do not duplicate, the upstream authoritative documents, the first walking slice, the second walking slice, and the third walking slice. Where an upstream constitutional principle, domain-model contract, context-map ownership, or prior-slice requirement already governs a behavior, the corresponding requirement here carries an explicit **Traceability** block and refines that behavior to the slice's scope. Where no upstream identifier exists, the requirement is flagged as a **Gap** for resolution before implementation. The slice continues the cumulative Gap numbering scheme established by Slices 1, 2, and 3: new gaps are recorded as **G-16** onward. The slice continues the cumulative Requirement numbering scheme established by Slice 3: new requirements are numbered starting at **Requirement 43**.

### Scope of this slice

In scope:

- Recording one `Measurement Definition` Resource (with revisions) that addresses one Intended Outcome Revision (Slice 2 Req 3) and declares what is to be measured, how often, in what unit, and over what observation window, by a Party holding `define_measurement` authority.
- Recording one or more native `Measurement Record` Immutable Records against a Measurement Definition Revision by a Party holding `record_measurement` authority.
- Recording one or more imported `Measurement Record` Immutable Records that preserve external authority designation per Principle 5.27.
- Recording one `Observed Outcome` Resource (with revisions) whose `outcome_kind = observed` and whose Revisions cite the supporting Measurement Records, by a Party holding `assess_outcome` authority.
- Recording one `Success-Condition Assessment` Immutable Record against the addressed Intended Outcome Revision and the sourced Observed Outcome Revision by a Party holding `assess_outcome` authority.
- Recording one `Outcome Review` Governance Decision Immutable Record that cites at least one Success-Condition Assessment and at least one Completion Record (Slice 3 Req 29) by a Party holding `issue_outcome_review` authority.
- Authorization-aware backlinks and forward-provenance navigation across the new outcome-measurement Resources and Records, joining seamlessly with the Slice 1 + Slice 2 + Slice 3 provenance chain back to the originating Decision and exact source Evidence.
- Audit records for every consequential outcome-measurement write and every denied unauthorized outcome-measurement attempt, written atomically with the originating write.
- Additive extension of the Slice 1 + Slice 2 + Slice 3 `slice-default-2026` disclosure policy to cover the new outcome-measurement node kinds.
- Additive extension of the Slice 1 + Slice 2 + Slice 3 authority enumeration with the four new authority types `define_measurement`, `record_measurement`, `assess_outcome`, `issue_outcome_review`, recorded as input to new backlog ADRs.
- One explainable Projection of current outcome status (for example, `Intended Outcome unmeasured`, `Intended Outcome measured`, `Intended Outcome observed`, `Intended Outcome success condition assessed`, `Intended Outcome reviewed`) derived from source Records, distinguished from authoritative Records per Principle 5.23 and from observed Outcome value per Principle 5.21.

Out of scope for this slice (deferred to later slices — see §"Out-of-Scope Boundaries"):

- Release 1E — Learning to Adaptation (`Learning Record`, `Adaptation Decision`, challenge of prior assumptions, supersession of Objective or Plan based on observed evidence).
- Slice 2 — Reproducible Publication of outcome-measurement artifacts.
- Slice 3 — Investment, cost, capacity, and portfolio reporting against outcome-measurement records (cost-per-outcome rollups, portfolio outcome aggregation, ROI computation).
- Automated statistical or causal inference over Measurement Records or Observed Outcomes (acknowledged as a Later story group in [`documents/07-user-story-map.md`](../../../documents/07-user-story-map.md) §7 and deferred).
- Multiple parallel Measurement Definitions per Intended Outcome (this slice permits one Measurement Definition Resource per Intended Outcome Revision; replacement Measurement Definitions are deferred to a later slice).
- Withdrawal, supersession, or correction of a recorded Measurement Record (a later governed supersession path is out of scope; superseded Observed Outcome Revisions are in scope).
- Conditional Outcome Review, two-of-N Outcome Review, or delegated Outcome Review; this slice records exactly one Outcome Review Authority per Outcome Review Record.
- Portability export of outcome-measurement Records or Resources.
- Real-time streaming of Measurement Records from external systems (imported Measurement Records are recorded synchronously on the Slice 4 endpoint; streaming is deferred).
- Automated Agent contribution provenance on outcome-measurement Records beyond recording that an authoring Party is human or automated.
- Any modification of Slice 1, Slice 2, or Slice 3 contexts (Identity, Audit, Authorization, Evidence, Knowledge, Trails, Provenance, Planning, Execution, Deliverable Repository) other than additive extensions defined in this document.

### Sub-systems

- **Walking_Slice_System**: The cumulative software realization of the first, second, third, and fourth walking slices. References to "the system" map to this term throughout this document.
- **Outcome_Service**: The new Slice 4 sub-system that records Measurement Definitions, Measurement Records, Observed Outcomes, Success-Condition Assessments, and Outcome Reviews. Owned by the Outcome Measurement and Learning bounded context per [`documents/03-context-map.md`](../../../documents/03-context-map.md) §2.8. The Outcome_Service is distinct from the Slice 2 Planning_Service and the Slice 3 Execution_Service: the Planning_Service records Intended Outcomes and Deliverable Expectations; the Execution_Service records Work Assignments, Productions, Milestone Acceptances, and Completions; the Outcome_Service records Measurements and Observed Outcomes and the reviews thereof. The four services SHALL NOT alias, mutate, or replace one another's Records.
- **Identity_Service**, **Authorization_Service**, **Audit_Log**, **Evidence_Repository**, **Knowledge_Service**, **Trail_Service**, **Provenance_Navigator**, **Planning_Service**, **Execution_Service**, **Deliverable_Repository**, **Disclosure_Policies registry**, **Interim_ADR_Records registry**: As defined in the [first-walking-slice requirements](../first-walking-slice/requirements.md) §"Glossary", the [second-walking-slice requirements](../second-walking-slice/requirements.md) §"Glossary", and the [third-walking-slice requirements](../third-walking-slice/requirements.md) §"Glossary". This slice reuses these sub-systems and SHALL NOT modify them except through additive extensions described in Requirements 52, 58, and 60.

### Resource kinds and Immutable Records introduced by this slice

- **Measurement Definition**: A Resource per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §3 whose Revisions per §4 record what is to be measured against an Intended Outcome Revision (Slice 2): the measurand description, the unit of measure, the observation-window descriptor, the cadence descriptor, the data-source descriptor, and the applicable scope. A Measurement Definition Resource has stable identity; its Revisions are immutable per §4 invariant 4.
- **Measurement Record**: An Immutable Record per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §8 that records one observed value against a Measurement Definition Revision. A Measurement Record carries an origin indicator drawn from `{native, imported}`. A Measurement Record is append-only per §8 invariant 2.
- **Imported Measurement Record**: A Measurement Record whose origin indicator is `imported`. An Imported Measurement Record additionally records the source-system identifier, the source-system record identifier, the source-system authority designation drawn from `{authoritative, replica, projection, index, federation}` per Principle 5.27, the source-system retrieval time, and the import time at the Outcome_Service.
- **Observed Outcome**: A Resource per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §3 whose Revisions per §4 record the assessed state of a target Intended Outcome Revision (Slice 2) based on cited Measurement Records. Every Observed Outcome Revision SHALL record `outcome_kind = observed` per §7.4 invariant 6 and SHALL distinguish itself from the Intended Outcome it addresses.
- **Success-Condition Assessment**: An Immutable Record per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §8.4 that records an assessment of whether the success-condition statement on the addressed Intended Outcome Revision (recorded under Slice 2 Requirement 3.2) is satisfied, partially satisfied, not satisfied, or unassessable, based on the cited Observed Outcome Revision. A Success-Condition Assessment is append-only per §8 invariant 2.
- **Outcome Review**: A Governance Decision Immutable Record per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §8.5 that records the Outcome Owner's published review of one Intended Outcome Revision after at least one Success-Condition Assessment has been recorded. An Outcome Review records the review-outcome category drawn from `{Achieved, Partially_Achieved, Not_Achieved, Inconclusive}`, the attribution stance drawn from `{Asserted, Partial, Unattributed, Contradicted}`, the confidence indicator drawn from `{High, Moderate, Low}`, the cited Success-Condition Assessment Record Identities, the cited Completion Record Identities (Slice 3 Req 29), and the cited produced Deliverable Revision Identities where applicable. An Outcome Review is immutable per §8.5 invariant 4.

### Roles introduced by this slice

The four roles below are **contextual roles** per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §9 and extend the contextual role catalog already established in Slices 1, 2, and 3. A Party holds a role only by virtue of an effective Role Assignment recorded by the Authorization_Service.

- **Measurement Designer**: A Party authorized to record a Measurement Definition Resource and Revisions against an Intended Outcome Revision within an applicable scope. Granted authorities include the new `define_measurement` authority type defined in Requirement 52. Measurement Designer authority SHALL NOT be substituted for Measurement Recorder, Outcome Assessor, or Outcome Reviewer authority and vice versa.
- **Measurement Recorder**: A Party authorized to record native Measurement Records and to record imported Measurement Records against a Measurement Definition Revision within an applicable scope. Granted authorities include the new `record_measurement` authority type defined in Requirement 52. Measurement Recorder authority SHALL NOT be substituted for Measurement Designer, Outcome Assessor, or Outcome Reviewer authority and vice versa.
- **Outcome Assessor**: A Party authorized to record an Observed Outcome Resource and Revisions and to record Success-Condition Assessment Immutable Records within an applicable scope. Granted authorities include the new `assess_outcome` authority type defined in Requirement 52. Outcome Assessor authority SHALL NOT be substituted for Measurement Designer, Measurement Recorder, or Outcome Reviewer authority and vice versa.
- **Outcome Reviewer**: A Party authorized to record an Outcome Review Governance Decision Immutable Record within an applicable scope. Granted authorities include the new `issue_outcome_review` authority type defined in Requirement 52. Outcome Reviewer authority SHALL NOT be substituted for Measurement Designer, Measurement Recorder, or Outcome Assessor authority and vice versa. The user-story-map roles "Outcome Owner" and "Outcome Analyst" are collapsed into the single contextual role "Outcome Reviewer" for the purposes of this slice; finer-grained outcome-review authority is deferred to a later slice.

### Slice-specific terms

- **Completed Plan Revision**: A Plan Revision for which a Completion Record has been finalized in Slice 3 per Slice 3 Requirement 29. A Completed Plan Revision is a permissible source for citing a Completion Record from an Outcome Review.
- **Target Intended Outcome Revision**: The Intended Outcome Revision named as the `Addresses` target of a Measurement Definition Revision, of an Observed Outcome Revision, and of a Success-Condition Assessment Record. The Target Intended Outcome Revision SHALL resolve in the Planning_Service at the recorded time of every Slice 4 write that references it.
- **Outcome Measurement Provenance Chain**: The end-to-end traversal Outcome Review → Success-Condition Assessment(s) → Observed Outcome Revision → Measurement Record(s) → Measurement Definition Revision → Intended Outcome Revision → Objective → Slice 1 Decision → Recommendation Revision → Finding Revision(s) → Content Region Occurrence(s) → Document Revision; with a parallel traversal Outcome Review → Cites → Completion Record(s) → Slice 3 Execution Provenance Chain → produced Deliverable Revision(s).
- **Slice 3 Completion Record**: A Completion Record produced by the third walking slice and conforming to its Requirement 29 (Record a Completion Record Against an Approved Plan Revision).


## Requirements

### Requirement 43: Durable Identity Foundation for Outcome Measurement Resources and Records

**User Story:** As an implementer of the fourth walking slice, I want every managed identity produced by the Outcome_Service to conform to the durable identity strategy already enforced by the Identity_Service, so that Measurement Definitions, Measurement Records, Observed Outcomes, Success-Condition Assessments, and Outcome Reviews remain referenceable across future bounded contexts, exports, and migrations on the same terms as Slice 1, Slice 2, and Slice 3 Resources.

**Traceability:**
- Constitution: Principle 5.5 (Identity is independent of location), Principle 5.6 (Durable states are historical).
- Domain model: §3 (Resource invariants), §4 (Resource Revision invariants), §8 (Immutable Record model), §8.4 (Audit Event analog), §8.5 (Governance Decision Immutable Record).
- Context map: §2.1 (Shared Graph Foundation), §2.8 (Outcome Measurement and Learning).
- User story map: §4 Release 1D.
- Slice 1: Requirement 1 (Durable Identity Foundation), AD-WS-2, AD-WS-3.
- Slice 2: Requirement 1 (Durable Identity Foundation for Planning Resources).
- Slice 3: Requirement 22 (Durable Identity Foundation for Execution Resources).
- Invariants: identity survives movement; identifier never reused; principal identifier is opaque; Observed Outcome Resource Identity and Revision Identity are distinct; Measurement Definition Resource Identity and Revision Identity are distinct.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Outcome_Service requests the Identity_Service to create a Measurement Definition Resource, a Measurement Definition Revision, a Measurement Record, an Observed Outcome Resource, an Observed Outcome Revision, a Success-Condition Assessment Record, or an Outcome Review Record, THE Identity_Service SHALL assign exactly one UUID version 7 identifier in canonical lowercase hyphenated 8-4-4-4-12 hex form, exactly once, before the entity becomes referenceable, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.1.
2. THE Identity_Service SHALL hold Measurement Definition Resource Identity and Measurement Definition Revision Identity as two distinct values for every Measurement Definition, and SHALL hold Observed Outcome Resource Identity and Observed Outcome Revision Identity as two distinct values for every Observed Outcome, each with cardinality one Resource Identity to one or more Revision Identities, and no Revision Identity shared across Resources, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.2 and Slice 2 Requirement 1.2.
3. WHEN an authorized actor renames or relocates a Measurement Definition Resource or an Observed Outcome Resource within the Outcome_Service, THE Identity_Service SHALL preserve the existing Resource Identity and every existing Revision Identity unchanged, generate no new Resource Identity, and replace no existing identity, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.3.
4. IF an identifier generation, import, or reference operation in the Outcome_Service would assign an existing identifier to different domain content, or would introduce a malformed identifier, THEN THE Identity_Service SHALL reject the operation, return an error indication identifying the conflicting identifier, leave the existing identifier bound to its original content unchanged, and append a Denial Record to the Audit_Log within the same operation, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.4.
5. WHEN the Provenance_Navigator resolves a Relationship from either its source endpoint or its target endpoint between any pair of slice Resources or Records (Slice 1, Slice 2, Slice 3, or Slice 4), THE Identity_Service SHALL return the same single authoritative Relationship Identity from both source-direction and backlink queries, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.5.
6. THE Identity_Service SHALL NOT reassign a once-assigned identifier to different domain content, even after Measurement Record recording, Observed Outcome Revision recording, Success-Condition Assessment finalization, Outcome Review finalization, retention expiry, or deletion of the original content, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.6.
7. THE Identity_Service SHALL NOT encode mutable name, repository path, organization name, security classification, lifecycle state, authority, semantic version, owning Party, source-system designation, or other business meaning into any issued identifier for any Outcome_Service Resource or Record, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.7.
8. THE Identity_Service SHALL hold Measurement Definition Resource Identity, Measurement Definition Revision Identity, Measurement Record Identity, Observed Outcome Resource Identity, Observed Outcome Revision Identity, Success-Condition Assessment Record Identity, and Outcome Review Record Identity as seven disjoint identifier roles relative to every Slice 1 identifier, every Slice 2 identifier, and every Slice 3 identifier; no Slice 4 identifier SHALL also identify a Slice 1, Slice 2, or Slice 3 entity, and no Slice 1, Slice 2, or Slice 3 identifier SHALL be reissued as a Slice 4 identifier.


### Requirement 44: Define a Measurement Definition for an Intended Outcome

**User Story:** As a Measurement Designer, I want to define a Measurement Definition that addresses an Intended Outcome Revision, so that what is to be observed is recorded explicitly, versionably, and separately from what was intended.

**Traceability:**
- Constitution: Principle 5.21 (Intent, Work, Output, and Outcome are Distinct), Principle 5.23 (Operational Events and Current Projections Are Distinct).
- Domain model: §3 (Resource invariants), §4 (Resource Revision invariants), §10.9 (Addresses Relationship).
- Context map: §2.5 (Work Planning) ↔ §2.8 (Outcome Measurement and Learning) — Outcome Measurement consumes Intended Outcomes from Work Planning.
- User story map: §4 Release 1D step 1; §3 row 7 (Measure Results).
- Slice 2: Requirement 3 (Define an Intended Outcome for an Objective) — the consumed source.
- Invariants: Measurement Definition Revisions are immutable; a Measurement Definition addresses exactly one Intended Outcome Revision; the success-condition statement on the addressed Intended Outcome Revision is not modified by recording a Measurement Definition.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN an authenticated Party holding effective `define_measurement` authority for the applicable scope submits a Measurement Definition creation request that names exactly one target Intended Outcome Revision Identity, THE Outcome_Service SHALL create a Measurement Definition Resource and an initial immutable Measurement Definition Revision within a nominal 5 seconds.
2. THE Outcome_Service SHALL record on every Measurement Definition Revision the Measurement Definition Resource Identity, the Measurement Definition Revision Identity, the target Intended Outcome Resource Identity, the target Intended Outcome Revision Identity, the measurand description text of 1 to 4,000 characters, the unit-of-measure descriptor of 1 to 200 characters, the observation-window descriptor of 1 to 1,000 characters, the cadence descriptor of 1 to 1,000 characters, the data-source descriptor of 1 to 1,000 characters, the authoring Party Identity, the applicable scope, the recorded time in UTC with millisecond precision, and exactly one `Addresses` Relationship from the Measurement Definition Revision to the target Intended Outcome Revision Identity per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §10.9.
3. THE Outcome_Service SHALL permit at most one Measurement Definition Resource per target Intended Outcome Resource Identity in this slice; a second Measurement Definition creation attempt against the same Intended Outcome Resource SHALL be rejected with no Measurement Definition Resource or Revision persisted. Replacement of an existing Measurement Definition Resource is deferred to a later slice.
4. IF a Measurement Definition creation request names a target Intended Outcome Revision Identity that does not resolve to an existing Intended Outcome Revision, names a target whose `outcome_kind` attribute is not the literal value `intended` per Slice 2 Requirement 3.2, omits any of the measurand description, unit-of-measure descriptor, observation-window descriptor, cadence descriptor, data-source descriptor, or applicable scope, names a target Intended Outcome whose Resource is already the target of a finalized Measurement Definition Resource (per criterion 3), or names more than one target Intended Outcome Revision, THEN THE Outcome_Service SHALL reject the action, decline to create any Measurement Definition Resource or Revision, and return an error indication identifying each invalid attribute.
5. IF the requesting Party is unauthenticated or does not hold effective `define_measurement` authority for the applicable scope at the recorded time, THEN THE Authorization_Service SHALL reject the action, the Outcome_Service SHALL decline to create any Measurement Definition Resource or Revision, and the Audit_Log SHALL append a Denial Record conforming to the Slice 1 AD-WS-9 disclosure policy as extended by Requirement 58.
6. WHEN a Measurement Definition Revision is finalized, THE Audit_Log SHALL append an immutable creation record identifying the Measurement Definition Resource Identity, Measurement Definition Revision Identity, target Intended Outcome Revision Identity, authoring Party Identity, and recorded time within 1 second of and in the same transaction as the Measurement Definition Revision creation, per Slice 1 Requirement 13.1.
7. IF an actor attempts to modify or delete a previously created Measurement Definition Revision or its `Addresses` Relationship, THEN THE Outcome_Service SHALL reject the operation, leave the Measurement Definition Revision byte-equivalent to its prior state, return an error indication identifying the immutability violation, and append a Denial Record to the Audit_Log conforming to Slice 1 Requirement 13.5.
8. WHEN the Outcome_Service creates a Measurement Definition Revision, THE Outcome_Service SHALL NOT modify, supersede, withdraw, or extend the target Intended Outcome Resource, target Intended Outcome Revision, the success-condition statement recorded on it under Slice 2 Requirement 3.2, the attribution-assumption text recorded on it under Slice 2 Requirement 3.2, or any other Slice 1, Slice 2, or Slice 3 row as a consequence of recording the Measurement Definition Revision.

### Requirement 45: Record a Native Measurement Record

**User Story:** As a Measurement Recorder, I want to record an observed value against a Measurement Definition Revision as a native Measurement Record, so that an observed value enters the system as a durable, append-only Immutable Record traceable to the definition that scoped it.

**Traceability:**
- Constitution: Principle 5.21, Principle 5.23, Principle 5.9 (Provenance Is Preserved End to End).
- Domain model: §8 (Immutable Record model).
- Context map: §2.8 (Outcome Measurement and Learning).
- User story map: §4 Release 1D step 2.
- Invariants: Measurement Records are append-only; a Measurement Record cites exactly one Measurement Definition Revision; the observed value is recorded as a quantity-and-unit pair consistent with the addressed Measurement Definition Revision; recording a Measurement Record does not modify the Measurement Definition Revision.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN an authenticated Party holding effective `record_measurement` authority for the applicable scope submits a native Measurement Record creation request that names exactly one target Measurement Definition Revision Identity and supplies an observed value, THE Outcome_Service SHALL create an immutable Measurement Record within a nominal 5 seconds.
2. THE Outcome_Service SHALL record on every native Measurement Record the Measurement Record Identity, the target Measurement Definition Resource Identity, the target Measurement Definition Revision Identity, the origin indicator with the literal value `native`, the observed value expressed as a decimal number with at most six fractional digits, the observed-value unit string matching the unit-of-measure descriptor recorded on the target Measurement Definition Revision per Requirement 44.2, the observation time in UTC with millisecond precision falling within the observation-window descriptor recorded on the target Measurement Definition Revision, the recording Party Identity, the applicable scope, the recorded time in UTC with millisecond precision, and exactly one `Cites` Relationship from the Measurement Record to the target Measurement Definition Revision Identity per [`documents/02-domain-model.md`](../../../documents/02-domain-model.md) §10.7.
3. IF a native Measurement Record creation request names a target Measurement Definition Revision Identity that does not resolve, supplies an observed value with more than six fractional digits, supplies an observed-value unit string that does not match the unit-of-measure descriptor on the target Measurement Definition Revision, supplies an observation time outside the observation-window descriptor recorded on the target Measurement Definition Revision, supplies an observation time later than the recorded time, omits the observed value, omits the observed-value unit, omits the observation time, omits the applicable scope, or supplies any attribute reserved for imported Measurement Records (source-system identifier, source-system record identifier, source-system authority designation, source-system retrieval time, import time), THEN THE Outcome_Service SHALL reject the action, decline to create any Measurement Record, and return an error indication identifying each invalid attribute.
4. IF the requesting Party is unauthenticated or does not hold effective `record_measurement` authority for the applicable scope at the recorded time, THEN THE Authorization_Service SHALL reject the action, the Outcome_Service SHALL decline to create any Measurement Record, and the Audit_Log SHALL append a Denial Record conforming to AD-WS-9 as extended by Requirement 58.
5. WHEN a native Measurement Record is finalized, THE Audit_Log SHALL append an immutable creation record identifying the Measurement Record Identity, target Measurement Definition Revision Identity, origin indicator, observed value, observed-value unit, observation time, recording Party Identity, and recorded time within 1 second of and in the same transaction as the Measurement Record creation, per Slice 1 Requirement 13.1.
6. IF an actor attempts to modify or delete a previously created Measurement Record, or to modify or delete its `Cites` Relationship to the Measurement Definition Revision, THEN THE Outcome_Service SHALL reject the operation, leave the Measurement Record byte-equivalent to its prior state, return an error indication identifying the immutability violation, and append a Denial Record to the Audit_Log conforming to Slice 1 Requirement 13.5.
7. IF the Audit_Log append for a Measurement Record creation fails, THEN THE Outcome_Service SHALL roll back the Measurement Record creation, decline to make the Measurement Record referenceable, and return an error indication identifying the audit append failure, per Slice 1 Requirement 13.6.

### Requirement 46: Record an Imported Measurement Record (Preserve External Authority)

**User Story:** As a Measurement Recorder, I want to record a Measurement Record imported from an external system of record while preserving the external authority designation and source-system identifiers, so that imported observations remain distinguishable from native ones and authority is never silently transferred.

**Traceability:**

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. **Intended-Outcome anchoring (invariant).** FOR ALL Measurement Definition Revisions recorded by the Outcome_Service, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: every Measurement Definition Revision has exactly one `Addresses` Relationship to an Intended Outcome Revision Identity that resolves in the Planning_Service and whose `outcome_kind` is `intended`. No Measurement Definition Revision exists without a matching Intended Outcome Revision.
2. **Outcome-Record authority (invariant).** FOR ALL Measurement Definition Revisions, the authoring Party held an effective Role Assignment whose granted authorities include `define_measurement` at the recorded time; FOR ALL native and imported Measurement Records, the recording Party held an effective Role Assignment whose granted authorities include `record_measurement` at the recorded time; FOR ALL Observed Outcome Revisions and Success-Condition Assessment Records, the authoring Party held an effective Role Assignment whose granted authorities include `assess_outcome` at the recorded time; FOR ALL Outcome Review Records, the reviewing Party held an effective Role Assignment whose granted authorities include `issue_outcome_review` at the recorded time. No outcome-measurement Record exists without a matching authority record.
3. **Authority non-substitution (invariant).** FOR ALL outcome-measurement Records, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: the twelve authority types `{view, modify, review, approve, assign, contribute, accept_milestone, complete, define_measurement, record_measurement, assess_outcome, issue_outcome_review}` are pairwise distinct in the Role Assignment evaluation function; no Measurement Definition exists whose authoring Party held only any single non-`define_measurement` authority among those twelve; no Measurement Record exists whose recording Party held only any single non-`record_measurement` authority among those twelve; no Observed Outcome Revision or Success-Condition Assessment exists whose authoring Party held only any single non-`assess_outcome` authority among those twelve; no Outcome Review Record exists whose reviewing Party held only any single non-`issue_outcome_review` authority among those twelve.
4. **Outcome-Record immutability (invariant).** FOR ALL Measurement Definition Revisions, Measurement Records, Observed Outcome Revisions, Success-Condition Assessment Records, and Outcome Review Records finalized at any observation point in the test session, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: at every later observation point in the test session, the Resource row (where applicable), the Revision or Record row, every constituent field, and every `Addresses` and `Cites` Relationship sourced from or targeting it are byte-equivalent to their state at first finalization.
5. **Intended/Observed separation enforced from outcome side (invariant).** FOR ALL outcome-measurement Resources, Revisions, and Records, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: no Measurement Definition, Measurement Record, Observed Outcome, Success-Condition Assessment, or Outcome Review carries an intended-side attribute (success-condition statement text, attribution-assumption text, plan-review outcome, plan-approval outcome, planned scope value, planned start date, planned end date) as a value on the row itself; and no Intended Outcome Resource, Deliverable Expectation Resource, Plan Revision, Plan Review Revision, Plan Approval Immutable Record, or execution Record has been mutated as a consequence of any Slice 4 action.
6. **Output is not Outcome — re-asserted from outcome side (invariant).** FOR ALL Outcome Review Records, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: no Outcome Review Record was created by automatic derivation from any Slice 3 Completion Record finalization or Milestone Acceptance finalization; every Outcome Review Record carries the explicit attribution stance attribute drawn from `{Asserted, Partial, Unattributed, Contradicted}`; an attribution stance of `Asserted` or `Contradicted` is accompanied by an attribution-evidence reference of at least one character.
7. **External-authority preservation for imported Measurement Records (invariant).** FOR ALL imported Measurement Records, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: the origin indicator is the literal `imported`; the source-system identifier, source-system record identifier, source-system authority designation, source-system retrieval time, and import time are all non-null and recorded as documented in Requirement 46.2; the source-system authority designation is drawn from `{authoritative, replica, projection, index, federation}`; the source-system retrieval time is not after the recorded time; and the (source-system identifier, source-system record identifier) pair is unique per target Measurement Definition Revision Identity per Requirement 46.3.
8. **Outcome Measurement Provenance Chain end-to-end (invariant).** FOR ALL Outcome Review Records whose entire Outcome Measurement Provenance Chain is visible to a requesting Party, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: traversal from the Outcome Review Record yields the ordered sequences Outcome Review → Success-Condition Assessment(s) → Observed Outcome Revision → Measurement Record(s) → Measurement Definition Revision → Intended Outcome Revision → Objective → Slice 1 Decision → Recommendation Revision → Finding Revision(s) → Content Region Occurrence(s) → Document Revision, and Outcome Review → Cites Completion Record(s) → Slice 3 Execution Provenance Chain → produced Deliverable Revision(s); every node identity in the returned chains resolves; the returned Content Region Occurrence span fields match the digest recorded on the Region Occurrence; and the chains are byte-equivalent across at least five repeated invocations of `navigate(outcome_review, party, t)` (idempotent retrieval).
9. **Indistinguishable denial for outcome-measurement endpoints (metamorphic).** FOR ALL Parties `P` and `P′` differing only in that `P′` lacks effective `define_measurement`, `record_measurement`, `assess_outcome`, or `issue_outcome_review` authority on some outcome-measurement target `R`, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: responses returned to `P′` for creation attempts on `R` are indistinguishable from responses produced when `R` does not exist, across observable channels result count, identifier set, ordering positions, pagination cursors, response size, error category, error wording, and latency (within 100 milliseconds variation).
10. **Backlink bidirectionality for outcome-measurement Resources (round-trip).** FOR ALL Relationships `R` recorded between outcome-measurement Resources or Records, between outcome-measurement Resources or Records and execution Records (Slice 3), between outcome-measurement Resources or Records and planning Resources (Slice 2), or between outcome-measurement Resources or Records and Slice 1 Resources, and FOR ALL requesting Parties `P` who hold view authority on both `R` and its source endpoint, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: the Provenance_Navigator returns `R` from the target's backlink query if and only if `R` is returned from the source's outbound query, and the Relationship attribute values returned from both directions are identical.
11. **Uniqueness of Measurement Definition and Outcome Review (invariant).** FOR ALL Intended Outcome Resource Identities created in any test session, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: at most one Measurement Definition Resource exists for a given target Intended Outcome Resource Identity; a second Measurement Definition creation attempt against the same Intended Outcome Resource is rejected with no Measurement Definition persisted. FOR ALL Intended Outcome Revision Identities created in any test session, at most one Outcome Review Record exists for a given target Intended Outcome Revision Identity; a second Outcome Review attempt against the same Intended Outcome Revision is rejected with no Outcome Review Record persisted. FOR ALL Measurement Definition Revision Identities, the pair (source-system identifier, source-system record identifier) is unique across imported Measurement Records.
12. **Slice 1, Slice 2, and Slice 3 non-modification (invariant).** FOR ALL test sessions exercising the Outcome_Service, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: at every observation point after any sequence of Slice 4 actions, every Audit_Records row, Identifier_Registry row, Interim_ADR_Records row, Disclosure_Policies row (apart from the additive extensions permitted by Slice 2 Requirement 17.5, Slice 3 Requirement 38.5, and Requirement 58.6 of this slice), Decisions row, Role_Assignments row (apart from the additive enumeration extensions permitted by Slice 2 Requirement 11.1, Slice 3 Requirement 32.1, and Requirement 52.1 of this slice), Document_Revisions row, Region_Occurrences row, Finding_Revisions row, Recommendation_Revisions row, Relationships row, Trail_Revisions row, Trail_Steps row, Provenance_Manifests row, Objective_Revisions row, Intended_Outcome_Revisions row, Project_Revisions row, Deliverable_Expectation_Revisions row, Activity_Plans row, Plan_Revisions row, Plan_Review_Revisions row, Plan_Approval_Records row, Work_Assignment_Records row, Work_Event_Records row, Time_Entry_Records row, Deliverable_Production_Records row, Milestone_Acceptance_Records row, Completion_Records row, Deliverable_Resources row, and Deliverable_Revisions row created by Slice 1, Slice 2, or Slice 3 is byte-equivalent to its state before the Slice 4 actions began.
13. **Identity uniqueness across slices (invariant).** FOR ALL identifiers issued by the Identity_Service in any test session covering all four slices, the Walking_Slice_System SHALL satisfy: identifiers are unique across all four slices and across every Resource kind and every Record kind, are in canonical UUIDv7 lowercase hyphenated form, and do not embed business metadata, per Slice 1 Requirement 1.1, Slice 2 Requirement 20.12, and Slice 3 Requirement 41.12.

If the Audit_Log append fails for any test-generated finalization, the originating finalization SHALL have been rolled back and SHALL NOT be observable from any query path.

## Gaps Flagged for Resolution

The following gaps were identified while reconciling this slice with the upstream documents, Slice 1, Slice 2, and Slice 3. They continue the cumulative Gap numbering scheme established by Slice 1 (G-1 through G-6, recorded in [`../first-walking-slice/requirements.md`](../first-walking-slice/requirements.md) §"Gaps Flagged for Resolution"), Slice 2 (G-6 through G-10, recorded in [`../second-walking-slice/requirements.md`](../second-walking-slice/requirements.md) §"Gaps Flagged for Resolution"), and Slice 3 (G-11 through G-15, recorded in [`../third-walking-slice/requirements.md`](../third-walking-slice/requirements.md) §"Gaps Flagged for Resolution"). New gaps start at **G-16**. They are recorded here so they can be addressed in the design phase rather than rediscovered during implementation.

1. **G-16 — Imported Measurement Record origin enumeration and source-system authority enumeration are undecided.** Requirement 46 requires every imported Measurement Record to carry an origin indicator drawn from `{native, imported}` and a source-system authority designation drawn from `{authoritative, replica, projection, index, federation}` per Principle 5.27. No upstream ADR yet enumerates the canonical source-system authority designations or governs additive expansion of the origin enumeration. The slice's design SHALL choose an interim representation (for example, extending the registered enumeration columns and adding registry rows covering the new values' semantics) and document the choice as input to a new backlog ADR (placeholder `ADR-HT-018`).
2. **G-17 — Authority enumeration extension to twelve types is undecided.** Requirement 52 requires four additive authority types `define_measurement`, `record_measurement`, `assess_outcome`, and `issue_outcome_review` alongside the Slice 1 + Slice 2 + Slice 3 enumeration `{view, modify, review, approve, assign, contribute, accept_milestone, complete}`. No upstream ADR yet enumerates the full canonical authority types or governs additive expansion beyond Slice 3's four values. The slice's design SHALL choose an interim representation consistent with G-11's resolution (Slice 3) and document the choice as input to a new backlog ADR (placeholder `ADR-HT-019`).
3. **G-18 — Disclosure policy extension to outcome-measurement node kinds and to imported source-system attributes is undecided.** Requirement 58 requires the `slice-default-2026` policy to be additively extended to cover the new outcome-measurement node kinds AND to treat the source-system identifier, source-system record identifier, source-system authority designation, source-system retrieval time, and import time on imported Measurement Records as restricted attributes per Principle 5.27. The mechanism chosen by Slice 2 (Gap G-7) and extended by Slice 3 (Gap G-12) is a candidate, but its applicability to per-attribute restriction on imported Measurement Records has not been validated. The slice's design SHALL choose an interim representation consistent with G-7's and G-12's resolutions and document the choice as input to a new backlog ADR (placeholder `ADR-HT-020`).
4. **G-19 — Relationship semantics for `Cites` between outcome-measurement Records and prior-slice Records are partially undecided.** Requirements 45, 47, 48, and 49 use `Cites` for Measurement Record → Measurement Definition Revision, Observed Outcome Revision → Measurement Record, Success-Condition Assessment → Observed Outcome Revision, Outcome Review → Success-Condition Assessment, Outcome Review → Completion Record, and Outcome Review → produced Deliverable Revision. The domain model permits each but does not prescribe a single canonical convention for these specific source/target/semantic_role triples. The slice's design SHALL choose the canonical Relationship Types and semantic role markers and document the choice as input to a new backlog ADR (placeholder `ADR-HT-021`).
5. **G-20 — Persistence representation for outcome-measurement Resources, Revisions, and Records is undecided.** Requirement 43 requires distinct Resource and Revision identity columns for Measurement Definitions and Observed Outcomes, and append-only identity for every Measurement Record, Success-Condition Assessment Record, and Outcome Review Record. The interim representation chosen by Slice 1 (insert-only tables with append-only triggers, AD-WS-4), extended by Slice 2 (Gap G-10), and extended by Slice 3 (Gap G-15) is the natural carrier, but the specific tables, columns, triggers, and Audit_Log atomic transactions for the seven new Record/Resource kinds have not been chosen. The slice's design SHALL choose the persistence representation, ensure it preserves the Slice 1, Slice 2, and Slice 3 append-only invariants, and document the choice as input to a new backlog ADR (placeholder `ADR-HT-022`).
